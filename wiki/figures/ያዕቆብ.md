## Biography

**ቅዱስ ያዕቆብ (Saint James the Greater)** was a fisherman of Galilee, the elder son of Zebedee, and the brother of **the Apostle John**. With his brother and Simon Peter, he was one of Christ's **three closest disciples** — the only ones present at the Transfiguration (ማቴ. 17:1–8), the raising of Jairus's daughter (ማር. 5:37), and the Agony in the Garden (ማቴ. 26:37).

Christ called him and his brother **"Boanerges"** — *"sons of thunder"* (ማር. 3:17) — for their fiery temperament. He once asked Christ to call down fire on a Samaritan village (ሉቃ. 9:54).

## Martyrdom

> «በዚያ ጊዜ — ንጉሥ ሄሮድስ ቤተክርስቲያንን ለማስጨነቅ — እጁን ዘረጋ።
> የዮሐንስ ወንድም ያዕቆብን — በሰይፍ ገደለው።»
> — የሐዋ. ሥራ 12:1–2

James was the **first apostle to be martyred** — beheaded by King Herod Agrippa I in Jerusalem around **44 AD**. He fulfilled the prophecy Christ gave him: *"You will indeed drink my cup"* (ማቴ. 20:23).

## Tradition

Tradition holds that James preached in Spain before returning to Jerusalem for his martyrdom. His relics rest at **Santiago de Compostela** in Spain — one of the three great pilgrimage destinations of medieval Christendom (with Rome and Jerusalem). The pilgrim road to Compostela has been walked by saints and sinners for over a thousand years.

He should not be confused with:
- **James the Less** (son of Alphaeus) — also an apostle.
- **James the Just** — "the brother of the Lord," first bishop of Jerusalem, author of the Epistle of James.
