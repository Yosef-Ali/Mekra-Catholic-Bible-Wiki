## Biography

**ኤርምያስ (Jeremiah)** was a priest's son from **Anathoth**, a village near Jerusalem. He was called by God to prophesy as a young man — *"Before I formed you in the womb I knew you"* (ኤር. 1:5) — and served for **40 years** through the final agony of the kingdom of Judah and the destruction of the Temple by Nebuchadnezzar in 587 BC.

He is called the **"weeping prophet"** because his book and the **Book of Lamentations** (traditionally his) record the deepest grief of the Old Testament — the destruction of the Holy City, the burning of the Temple, and the exile of the people.

> «በዓይኖቼ ውስጥ ከሁለት ምንጭ ያለ ውሃ ቢወርድ — በቀንና በሌሊት — ስለ ሕዝቤ ሴት ልጅ የተገደሉ ሰዎች ላለቅስባቸው።»
> — ኤር. 9:1

He was repeatedly imprisoned, beaten, thrown into a cistern, and persecuted by his own people for telling them that resistance to Babylon was futile and that God's judgment was coming. After Jerusalem fell, he was forcibly taken to Egypt where, tradition says, **his own people stoned him to death**.

## The New Covenant prophecy

Jeremiah's most important contribution to Christian theology is the prophecy of a **new covenant**:

> «እነሆ — የእስራኤል ቤትና የይሁዳ ቤት ጋር — አዲስ ቃል ኪዳን አደርጋለሁ።
> ከአባቶቻቸው ጋር እንዳደረግሁት ቃል ኪዳን አይደለም …
> ሕጌን በውስጣቸው ካስቀመጥሁ — በልባቸው ከጽፍኩ — ብኋላ — እኔ አምላክ እሆናቸዋለሁ።»
> — ኤር. 31:31–33

This is **the** Old Testament passage Christ fulfilled at the Last Supper:

> «ይህ ለኃጢአት ስርየት ስለብዙዎች የሚፈስ የአዲስ ኪዳን ደሜ ነው።»
> — ማቴ. 26:28

Christ takes Jeremiah's promise and applies it to his own blood. The **New Covenant** is the central category of Catholic theology of the Eucharist (see [[teaching/eucharist]]) and the Christian life.

## Other Christological themes

- **Lambs led to slaughter** (ኤር. 11:19) — applied to Christ.
- **The thirty pieces of silver** (ዘካ. 11:12–13, attributed in ማቴ. 27:9 to Jeremiah).
- **The voice in Ramah weeping** (ኤር. 31:15) — fulfilled in the slaughter of the Holy Innocents (ማቴ. 2:18).

## Lamentations — the Triduum reading

The **Lamentations of Jeremiah** are read at the Catholic and Tewahedo liturgies of **Holy Week** — the dirge of Jerusalem becoming the dirge of Christ on the cross.

> «እናንተ በመንገድ የምታልፉ ሁሉ ሁኔታ — ተመልከቱ — የእኔን ሥቃይ የመሰለ ሥቃይ የለም።»
> — ሰቆቃው ኤር. 1:12

The Catholic *Tenebrae* service of Holy Week is built around these chanted laments.

## A patron for the persecuted faithful

Jeremiah is the patron of those who must speak unwelcome truth — and of those who do so at great personal cost. His example teaches:

- **Faithfulness is more important than success.** Jeremiah warned for forty years and was ignored. He died in exile. By worldly measure, his life was a failure — yet his words are eternal Scripture.
- **Tears are holy.** The "weeping prophet" gives the catechist permission to mourn. There is no false bravado in true faith.
- **The new covenant is internal.** What Israel could not do externally, Christ does in the heart.
