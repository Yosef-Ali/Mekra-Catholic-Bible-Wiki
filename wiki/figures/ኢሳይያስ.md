# ነቢዩ ኢሳይያስ — The Prophet Isaiah

**Type:** figure
**Amharic:** ነቢዩ ኢሳይያስ
**Variants:** Yesha'yahu (יְשַׁעְיָהוּ — "the Lord is salvation")
**English:** Isaiah

## Biography

**ኢሳይያስ (Isaiah)** is the **prince of the prophets** — author of the longest and most theologically rich book of OT prophecy. He prophesied in **Jerusalem** through some of the most turbulent years of the Davidic kingdom, witnessing:

- The death of King Uzziah (the year of his great vision: ኢሳ. 6:1).
- The Assyrian invasion under Sennacherib (701 BC) — and Jerusalem's miraculous deliverance.
- The reign of righteous King Hezekiah, his close counselor.

Tradition records that he was **martyred under King Manasseh** — sawn in two inside a hollow tree (the apocryphal *Ascension of Isaiah*; alluded to in ዕብ. 11:37: *"They were sawn in two"*).

## The five great Christological prophecies

Isaiah is called the **"Fifth Gospel"** for the precision of his foretelling of Christ:

### 1. Virgin Birth — ኢሳ. 7:14
> «እነሆ — ድንግል ትፀንሳለች — ወንድ ልጅም ትወልዳለች — ስሙንም ዐማኑኤል — ብለሽ ትጠራዋለች።»

Quoted in ማቴ. 1:23 — fulfilled in the conception of Christ from the Virgin.

### 2. Light to the Gentiles — ኢሳ. 9:1–2
> «በጨለማ የሚሄድ ሕዝብ — ታላቅ ብርሃን አየ።»

Quoted in ማቴ. 4:14–16 — the start of Christ's Galilean ministry.

### 3. Names of the Messiah — ኢሳ. 9:6
> «ስሙም — የተገለጠ መካር — ኃያል አምላክ — የዘላለም አባት — የሰላም አለቃ — ይባላል።»

The prophetic naming of the Messiah as **God himself**.

### 4. The Suffering Servant — ኢሳ. 53 (the heart of the Fifth Gospel)
> «ለበደላችን ቆሰለ — ለሥልጣናችን ተቀጠቀጠ … ዝምታውን ጠብቆ ነበር።»

The fourth Servant Song — read at every Catholic Good Friday liturgy. The suffering, death, and burial of Christ foretold in seven hundred years before the event.

### 5. Universal kingdom — ኢሳ. 11:1–10
> «ከእሴይ ጉቶ ዘር ይወጣል … የእግዚአብሔር መንፈስ ያርፍበታል — የጥበብና የማስተዋል መንፈስ።»

The seven gifts of the Spirit (see [[teaching/gifts-of-holy-spirit]]) prophesied as resting on the Messiah.

## Isaiah and Mary

Catholic Marian devotion has always read Isaiah's *"Behold, a virgin shall conceive"* (7:14) as the **first explicit prophecy of the Mother of God**. Mary's "Behold the handmaid of the Lord" (ሉቃ. 1:38) answers Isaiah's "Behold, a virgin" — separated by 700 years and bound by the same Spirit.

## In the Liturgy

Isaiah is read at every Mass during **Advent** — the prophet of expectation par excellence. His vision of Christ in glory (ኢሳ. 6:1–8) gives the Church the **Sanctus** of every Mass:

> «ቅዱስ — ቅዱስ — ቅዱስ — የሠራዊት ጌታ።»

What the seraphim sang in Isaiah's vision, the Church sings at every Eucharist.
