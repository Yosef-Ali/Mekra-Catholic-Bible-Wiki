## Biography

**ቅዱስ እንድርያስ (Saint Andrew)** was a Galilean fisherman, the brother of **Simon Peter**, and a former disciple of **John the Baptist**. He has the singular honor of being **the first-called** of the twelve apostles — the *Protokletos* in the Eastern Christian tradition.

When John the Baptist pointed to Christ and said *"Behold the Lamb of God,"* Andrew followed Jesus, spent the day with him, and immediately ran to find his brother:

> «መሲሕን አግኝተናል!»
> — ዮሐ. 1:41

He brought Peter to Christ. Without Andrew's first witness, Peter — the rock of the Church — might never have come.

## Notable moments in the Gospels

- **The feeding of the 5,000** (ዮሐ. 6:8–9) — Andrew is the one who brings forward the boy with five loaves and two fish.
- **The Greeks who wished to see Jesus** (ዮሐ. 12:20–22) — they approach Philip, who tells Andrew, who tells Jesus. Andrew is the **bridge-builder**.
- **At Pentecost and after** — listed among the Twelve, present at the descent of the Spirit.

## Mission and martyrdom

Tradition records that Andrew preached in **Scythia, Greece, and Asia Minor**. He was crucified at Patras in Achaia (Greece) on an **X-shaped cross** (the *crux decussata*, now called *Saint Andrew's Cross*) — having begged not to be crucified upright like his Lord, considering himself unworthy.

His relics rest in part at Patras and partly at the cathedral of Amalfi, Italy.

## Patronage

- **Patron of Scotland** (the Scottish flag bears the Saint Andrew's Cross — the *Saltire*)
- **Patron of Greece, Russia, Romania, and Ukraine**
- **Patron of fishermen**
- **Patron of singers**
