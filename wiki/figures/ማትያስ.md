## Biography

**ቅዱስ ማትያስ (Saint Matthias)** is unique among the Twelve — he is the **only apostle not personally chosen by Christ during his earthly ministry**. He was selected by lot, after prayer, to fill the place left empty by **Judas Iscariot's** betrayal and death.

The episode is recorded in **Acts 1:15–26**, the first apostolic act after the Ascension and before Pentecost.

> «ጴጥሮስ ቆሞ — በወንድሞቹ መካከል — እንዲህ አለ — "ወንድሞች ሆይ — ስለ ይሁዳ — መንፈስ ቅዱስ በዳዊት አፍ እስከተናገረ ያለው የመጽሐፍ ቃል መፈጸም ነበረበት…"»
> — የሐዋ. ሥራ 1:15–16

Peter explains the necessity: Judas's betrayal and self-destruction had created an absence in the apostolic college that **must be filled**. The qualifications are stated:

> «ከዚህ የምርጫ መካከል ካሉት ሰዎች — ጌታ ኢየሱስ ከእኛ ጋር በነበረበት ጊዜ ሁሉ ከእኛ ጋር ካደሩትና ካገለገሉት ሰዎች — ከዮሐንስ ጥምቀት ጀምሮ — እስከ ዕርገቱ ድረስ — ከእነዚህ ሰዎች መካከል አንድ ሰው — የትንሣኤው ምስክር — መሆን አለበት።»
> — የሐዋ. ሥራ 1:21–22

The apostles propose two candidates — **Joseph called Barsabbas (also called Justus)** and **Matthias**. They pray:

> «ጌታ ሆይ — የሁላችንን ልብ የምታውቅ — ከእነዚህ ሁለት — የመረጥከውን አሳየን።»
> — የሐዋ. ሥራ 1:24

They cast lots. The lot falls on Matthias.

> «በዐሥራ አንዱ ሐዋርያት ላይ ተጨመረ።»
> — የሐዋ. ሥራ 1:26

## Theological significance — apostolic succession

This is one of the most important passages in the New Testament for the doctrine of **apostolic succession** ([[apologetics/apostolic-succession]]). Notice what the Church does:

1. **Recognizes the apostolic office must be filled** — the Twelve are not a closed coincidence but a continuing reality.
2. **Sets clear qualifications** — eyewitness companionship, recognized witness.
3. **Prays for divine guidance.**
4. **Acts collegially** — through the gathered community.
5. **Confirms by ritual** — Matthias is *numbered with* the Eleven.

The same pattern continues to the present day: when a bishop dies, the office must be filled. The Catholic Church's first act after the Ascension was to ensure apostolic continuity. **This is not optional theology — it is apostolic practice from day one.**

## Mission and martyrdom

The New Testament records nothing about Matthias's later ministry. Tradition variously places his mission in **Cappadocia, Ethiopia, or the Caspian region**. Some traditions hold that he was martyred in **Jerusalem by stoning** (then beheaded); others record death by crucifixion in **Colchis** (modern Georgia).

Some Ethiopian traditions name Matthias as a missionary in Ethiopia or its neighboring regions — one of the threads through which Christianity may have first reached the kingdom of Aksum even before St. Frumentius (አባ ሰላማ) founded the formal Ethiopian episcopate in the 4th century.

## What Matthias teaches

Matthias is the patron of:
- **Carpenters**
- **The replacement / understudy** — the one called when the first has fallen
- **Recovering alcoholics** (some traditions)

His life teaches the Christian:

- **God uses the unseen.** Matthias spent three years walking with Christ unnoticed in the Gospels, and yet was chosen.
- **The Church survives betrayal.** Judas fell. Christ's plan did not.
- **Lots cast in prayer become divine choice.** What seems random to us is shepherded by Providence.
