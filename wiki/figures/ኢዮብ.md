## Biography

**ኢዮብ (Job)** was a wealthy patriarch from the land of **Uz** — east of Israel, possibly in modern Edom or Arabia. The book describes him as *"blameless and upright, fearing God and turning away from evil"* (ኢዮብ 1:1). He had seven sons, three daughters, vast herds, and many servants.

Tradition (preserved in the **Septuagint addition** to Job and in the *Testament of Job*) identifies him as a non-Israelite — possibly the Edomite king Jobab (ዘፍ. 36:33). Catholic tradition has always reverenced him as a **gentile righteous one** — proof that righteousness is possible outside the chosen line.

## The book's structure

The Book of Job is one of the masterpieces of world literature — and the Bible's most concentrated meditation on **innocent suffering**.

1. **Prologue (chs. 1–2)** — the heavenly council; Satan is permitted to test Job.
2. **Job's lament (ch. 3)** — he curses the day of his birth.
3. **The three friends** — Eliphaz, Bildad, Zophar — argue that Job must have sinned (chs. 4–31).
4. **Elihu's intervention** (chs. 32–37) — a fourth friend, younger and more nuanced.
5. **God speaks from the whirlwind** (chs. 38–41) — overwhelming Job with questions about creation rather than answers about suffering.
6. **Job's response** (ch. 42:1–6): *"I have heard of you with the hearing of the ear, but now my eye sees you. Therefore I despise myself and repent in dust and ashes."*
7. **Restoration** — God restores Job double what he had lost.

## The deepest lines

### The patience of Job (ኢዮብ 1:21)

> «ራቁቴን ከእናቴ ሆድ ወጥቻለሁ — ራቁቴን ወደዚያ እመለሳለሁ።
> እግዚአብሔር ሰጠ — እግዚአብሔር ወሰደ።
> የእግዚአብሔር ስም የተባረከ ይሁን።»

This verse is sung at every Catholic funeral.

### The Redeemer prophecy (ኢዮብ 19:25–27)

> «እኔ — መድኃኒቴ ሕያው እንደሆነ — ዐውቃለሁ።
> በመጨረሻው ዘመን — በምድር ላይ ይቆማል።
> ሥጋዬ ቢበሰብስም — በሥጋዬ ግን — እግዚአብሔርን አያለሁ።»

This is one of the **clearest Old Testament prophecies of the Resurrection** — and Christian tradition reads it as Job's confession of faith in **Christ the Redeemer** (Latin *Redemptor*) and the future bodily resurrection. It is set to music in Handel's *Messiah* — *"I know that my Redeemer liveth."*

### God's answer from the whirlwind (ኢዮብ 38:4)

> «የምድሩን መሠረት ስጥል — የት ነበርሽ?
> አስተውለሽ ከሆነ — ንገሪኝ።»

God does not answer Job's *why* — he answers with *who*. The mystery of suffering is not resolved by argument but by encounter with the Creator.

## What Job teaches

The Book of Job is the Old Testament's deepest treatment of:

- **Innocent suffering.** Job is righteous yet suffers. The friends' theology (suffering = punishment) is wrong. God himself rebukes them (42:7).
- **The limits of human understanding.** The mystery of evil exceeds our reasoning.
- **Faith in darkness.** Job continues to address God even when accusing him: *"Though he slay me, yet will I trust in him"* (13:15).
- **Christ prefigured.** The just man who suffers innocently and is finally vindicated is the Old Testament shadow of Christ. The Suffering Servant of Isaiah and the Job of the Wisdom literature meet in Christ on the cross.

## In Catholic Liturgy

- Job 19:25–27 is read at funerals — proclaiming the resurrection in the face of death.
- Job 1:21 is the response of every Catholic funeral.
- Job is read in the **Office of the Dead** in the Liturgy of the Hours.
- Holy Week readings draw deeply on Job alongside Lamentations and the Servant Songs of Isaiah.
