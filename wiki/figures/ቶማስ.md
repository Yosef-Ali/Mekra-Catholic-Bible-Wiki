## Biography

**ቅዱስ ቶማስ (Saint Thomas)** — also called **Didymus** ("the Twin") — appears in the Gospels as a man of bold loyalty and demanding honesty. He is most often remembered for his moment of doubt — but he is equally the **first man to confess the divinity of the risen Christ**.

## The three Thomas moments in John

### 1. *"Let us also go, that we may die with him"* (ዮሐ. 11:16)

When Christ resolves to go to Bethany — into territory where the Jews want to stone him — the other apostles try to dissuade him. Thomas alone says: *"Let us go too, that we may die with him."* This is courage, not doubt.

### 2. *"How can we know the way?"* (ዮሐ. 14:5)

At the Last Supper, when Christ says *"You know the way where I am going,"* Thomas is the one who admits honestly: *"Lord, we don't know where you are going. How can we know the way?"* This honest question elicits one of the greatest sayings of Christ:

> «መንገድና እውነት ሕይወትም እኔ ነኝ።»
> — ዮሐ. 14:6

### 3. *"My Lord and my God"* (ዮሐ. 20:24–29)

Thomas was absent the first time the risen Christ appeared. He refused to believe on others' word: *"Unless I see in his hands the print of the nails… I will not believe."* Eight days later, Christ appeared again, this time inviting Thomas to touch his wounds. Thomas's response is the apex of Christological confession:

> «ጌታዬ — አምላኬም።»
> — ዮሐ. 20:28

This is the **first explicit confession of Christ as God** in the Gospels — from the doubter, who became the first proclaimer of Christ's divinity.

## Mission to India

Strong tradition — preserved among the **Saint Thomas Christians** of South India to this day — holds that Thomas evangelized as far east as **India**, founding seven churches in Kerala. He was martyred at **Mylapore (modern Chennai)** around 72 AD, killed with a lance.

The St. Thomas Christians of Kerala have continuously practiced Christianity since this apostolic mission — the oldest unbroken Christian community outside Palestine, alongside Egypt and Ethiopia.

## A patron for the doubter

Thomas is the patron of those who struggle to believe — and of architects, builders, and surveyors (from the tradition that he built a palace for an Indian king).

His example reminds the Christian: **honest doubt brought to Christ ends in deeper faith**. Thomas did not pretend to believe what he did not. He brought his doubt directly into Christ's presence — and Christ honored that honesty with the gift of certainty.
