## Biography

**ቅዱስ በርተሎሜዎስ (Saint Bartholomew)** is widely identified — by ancient and modern tradition alike — with **Nathanael** of John's Gospel. The name *"Bartholomew"* is Aramaic *Bar-Talmai* — "son of Talmai" — a patronymic; **Nathanael** is the personal name. The Synoptics list him as Bartholomew, John as Nathanael, and the lists pair him in both with **Philip**.

His introduction to Christ is one of the loveliest scenes in the Gospels:

> «ፊልጶስ ናትናኤልን አግኝቶ — "በሙሴ ኦሪትና በነቢያት የተጻፈውን — ናዝሬታዊውን ኢየሱስን — የዮሴፍን ልጅ — አግኝተነዋል" አለው።
> ናትናኤል ግን — "ከናዝሬት ምን መልካም ሊመጣ ይችላል?" አለው።
> ፊልጶስ — "መጥተህ እይ።"»
> — ዮሐ. 1:45–46

Christ saw Nathanael coming and said:

> «እነሆ — በውስጡ ተንኮል የሌለበት — እውነተኛ እስራኤላዊ።»
> — ዮሐ. 1:47

When Nathanael asked *"How do you know me?"*, Christ answered: *"Before Philip called you, when you were under the fig tree, I saw you."* The seeing-through-walls of the Lord moved Nathanael to instant confession:

> «መምህር — አንተ የእግዚአብሔር ልጅ ነህ — የእስራኤልም ንጉሥ።»
> — ዮሐ. 1:49

## Mission

Tradition records Bartholomew's missionary travels through **India, Mesopotamia, and Armenia**. He is especially honored as the **apostle to Armenia**, which traditionally credits him (along with Saint Thaddeus) as its evangelizer — making the Armenian Apostolic Church one of the oldest in the world.

## Martyrdom

He was martyred at **Albanopolis in Armenia** — by the gruesome death of being **flayed alive** before being crucified. This is why Christian iconography depicts him holding his own skin and a knife — most famously in Michelangelo's Sistine Chapel *Last Judgment*, where Bartholomew's flayed skin bears Michelangelo's own self-portrait.

## A patron for honest hearts

Bartholomew is the patron of:
- **Tanners and butchers** (because of his manner of death)
- **Plasterers**
- **Armenia**

His example is the **transparent heart**: Christ called him *"a true Israelite in whom there is no guile."* The catechist can hold him up as the model of the Christian who is the same in private and in public, in word and in thought.
