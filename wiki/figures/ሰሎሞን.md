# ንጉሥ ሰሎሞን — King Solomon the Wise

**Type:** figure
**Amharic:** ንጉሥ ሰሎሞን
**Variants:** Shlomo (שְׁלֹמֹה — "peaceful"), Jedidiah (*"beloved of the Lord"* — his name from God)
**English:** Solomon
**Role:** Third king of Israel, builder of the First Temple, author of Wisdom literature, traditional ancestor of the Ethiopian Solomonic dynasty
**Traditional dates:** c. 970–931 BC
**Feast day:** June 25 in some Eastern traditions
**Sources:** 5
**Last updated:** 2026-05-09

## Biography

**ሰሎሞን (Solomon)** was the **second son of David and Bathsheba**, born after the death of their first child. God called him *"Jedidiah"* — *"beloved of the Lord"* (2ኛ ሳሙ. 12:25). He succeeded David as the **third king of Israel** at a young age, ruling for **40 years** over the united kingdom at its zenith.

His reign was marked by **peace, prosperity, and unprecedented wisdom**. He built the **First Temple of Jerusalem** — the dwelling place of God among Israel — completing in 7 years (c. 957 BC) what his father had prepared. The Temple stood for 410 years until destroyed by Nebuchadnezzar in 587 BC.

## The dream at Gibeon

Early in his reign, God appeared to Solomon in a dream and offered him whatever he asked. Solomon's reply set the pattern for all biblical wisdom:

> «አሁን ጌታ አምላኬ — በአባቴ በዳዊት ምትክ ባሪያህን አንግሰህኛል።
> እኔ ግን ትንሽ ልጅ ነኝ — መውጫና መግቢያን አላውቅም።
> ለዚህም — ሕዝብህን — እንዲፈርድ — ሊረዳው — ለማስተዋል ልብ ስጥ።»
> — 1ኛ ነገሥ. 3:7–9

God was so pleased with the request that he gave Solomon not only wisdom but also the riches and honor he had not asked for.

> «የቀድሞዎች ሁሉ የሌላቸው እና የኋለኛዎች ሁሉ የማይኖራቸው — ጥበበኛና ብልህ ልብ ሰጥቼሃለሁ።»
> — 1ኛ ነገሥ. 3:12

## The wisdom literature

Tradition attributes to Solomon **five books of the Old Testament** in whole or part:

1. **Proverbs (ምሳሌ)** — the practical wisdom of daily life.
2. **Ecclesiastes (መክብብ)** — the meditation on vanity and the meaning of life: *"Vanity of vanities, all is vanity."*
3. **Song of Songs (መኃልየ መኃልይ)** — the wedding song of love between God and his people; the most sublime poetry in Scripture.
4. **Wisdom of Solomon (ጥበበ ሰሎሞን)** — deuterocanonical; one of the books rejected by the Reformers (see [[apologetics/deuterocanon]]).
5. **Psalms** — two psalms (72, 127) bear his name.

Solomon's reputation became so great that *"the queen of the South"* — the **Queen of Sheba (ንግሥት ሳባ)** — came from her kingdom to see his wisdom. Christ himself appealed to this episode (ማቴ. 12:42).

## The Queen of Sheba and Ethiopian tradition

The visit of the **Queen of Sheba** to Solomon (1ኛ ነገሥ. 10:1–13) is the foundational episode of Ethiopian Christian and royal identity. The **Kebra Nagast** — *"The Glory of Kings"* — the 14th-century Ethiopian national epic, develops the tradition that:

- The Queen returned to her kingdom carrying Solomon's son.
- This son, **Menelik I**, founded the **Solomonic dynasty** of Ethiopia.
- Menelik later traveled to Jerusalem and brought the **Ark of the Covenant** to Aksum, where Ethiopian Tewahedo Christians believe it remains to this day.

The Ethiopian imperial line — until the deposition of Haile Selassie in 1974 — claimed unbroken descent from Solomon. **The Ethiopian flag, the Ethiopian Church's iconography, and Ethiopian national identity** are deeply Solomonic. Job and Solomon are the two non-Israelite (or trans-national) figures whom Ethiopia has most cherished as its own.

## Solomon's failure

The book of Kings does not flatter Solomon. In his old age, he **violated the Mosaic law** in three specific ways (cf. ዘዳ. 17:14–17):

- **Multiplied wives** — 700 wives and 300 concubines (1ኛ ነገሥ. 11:3).
- **Multiplied wealth** — vast accumulation.
- **Multiplied horses** — including imports from Egypt.

His foreign wives turned his heart toward their gods. He built shrines to Chemosh, Molech, and Astarte. As a result, God divided the kingdom after his death — leaving only Judah to his son Rehoboam, while ten tribes followed Jeroboam into the Northern Kingdom of Israel.

The Bible's portrait is dual: **wisest of kings — and ultimately a fool**. The book of Ecclesiastes is read by some Fathers as Solomon's repentance in old age — looking back on a life that pursued every pleasure and found *vanity*.

## What Solomon teaches

- **Wisdom is the first gift to ask for.** Everything else is dust by comparison.
- **The greatest wisdom can fail.** Holiness is a daily discipline, not a one-time gift.
- **Compromise with the world's gods** — through marriage, alliance, or appetite — corrupts even the wisest heart.
- **The Temple Solomon built** prefigured Christ's body, the new Temple (ዮሐ. 2:19–21), and the Church.

Christ himself drew the contrast: *"Behold, something greater than Solomon is here"* (ማቴ. 12:42). The fullness of wisdom is the Word of God himself — not even Solomon at his height.
