# ነቢዩ ዳንኤል — The Prophet Daniel

**Type:** figure
**Amharic:** ነቢዩ ዳንኤል
**Variants:** Daniyyel (דָּנִיֵּאל — "God is my judge"), Belteshazzar (his Babylonian name)
**English:** Daniel
**Role:** Prophet, courtier in Babylon and Persia, seer of apocalyptic visions
**Traditional dates:** c. 620–530 BC
**Feast day:** July 21 (ሐምሌ 14)
**Sources:** 4

## Biography

**ዳንኤል (Daniel)** was carried into **Babylonian exile** as a young noble of Judah in the first deportation (605 BC). Trained in the wisdom of the Chaldeans, he served at the Babylonian court under **Nebuchadnezzar** and continued under successive kings until the **Persian** conquest, serving **Cyrus the Great**. His career spanned nearly the entire 70 years of the exile.

He refused to defile himself with the king's food (ዳን. 1:8) and was given by God *"understanding of all visions and dreams"* (ዳን. 1:17). Three times he risked his life rather than compromise his faith — and three times God delivered him.

## The four great deliverances

### 1. The fiery furnace (ዳን. 3) — three companions

His three friends **Shadrach, Meshach, and Abednego** (Hebrew names: Hananiah, Mishael, Azariah) were thrown into a furnace heated seven times more than usual for refusing to worship Nebuchadnezzar's golden image. They walked unharmed in the fire — joined by *"a fourth, like a son of the gods"* (ዳን. 3:25).

In the **deuterocanonical addition** (ዳን. 3:24–90 — the **Prayer of Azariah** and the **Song of the Three Young Men**), the youths sing one of the most beloved hymns in Christian liturgy: *"Bless the Lord, all you works of the Lord."*

### 2. The lions' den (ዳን. 6)

For praying three times a day toward Jerusalem in defiance of King Darius's decree, Daniel was thrown into a den of lions. God **"shut the lions' mouths"** (ዳን. 6:22) — and the king who tried to save him pulled him out unharmed the next morning, then commanded all his subjects to fear the God of Daniel.

### 3. Susanna and the elders (ዳን. 13 — deuterocanonical)

The young Daniel exposes two corrupt judges who falsely accused the chaste Susanna of adultery. By cross-examination he reveals their inconsistency, vindicates the innocent, and condemns the guilty. A foundational text on **judicial integrity** in Christian tradition.

### 4. Bel and the Dragon (ዳን. 14 — deuterocanonical)

Daniel exposes the priests of Bel as frauds (they were eating the offerings themselves) and slays the dragon worshipped by the Babylonians. The episode is a comic gem of Old Testament anti-idolatry.

## The apocalyptic visions

The second half of Daniel (chapters 7–12) contains some of the most influential **apocalyptic visions** in the Bible:

### The four beasts and the Son of Man (ዳን. 7)

> «በሌሊት ራዕይም አይቼ ነበር — ከሰማይ ደመና ጋር — ሰው ልጅ የመሰለ መጣ።
> ወደ መጨረሻው ዘመን ሊቀቦ ቀረበ።
> ለእርሱም ሥልጣን — ክብር — መንግሥት ተሰጠ።»
> — ዳን. 7:13–14

Christ adopted the title **"Son of Man"** from this passage. His use of it before the Sanhedrin (ማቴ. 26:64) is what they took as blasphemy worthy of death — because they understood Daniel's vision: the Son of Man is God's own.

### The 70 weeks (ዳን. 9:24–27)

A timetable for the coming of the Anointed One — read by the early Church as fulfilled in Christ.

### The resurrection of the dead (ዳን. 12:2)

> «ብዙ የሚተኙት — ከምድር አፈር — ይነሣሉ።
> እነሱም — ለዘላለም ሕይወት — ሌሎቹም ለዘላለም ውርደት።»

This is the **clearest Old Testament prophecy of bodily resurrection and final judgment** — a doctrine the Pharisees of Christ's time accepted (and the Sadducees rejected) on the basis of this verse.

## In Christian tradition

Daniel is the patron of:
- **Prisoners** (those in dens not of their making)
- **Translators and interpreters** (gift for visions)
- **Those facing impossible odds**

The Christian tradition has read Daniel especially in the light of:
- **Christ as Son of Man.**
- **Apocalyptic literature** (the Revelation borrows its imagery wholesale from Daniel).
- **The deuterocanonical hymns** — the *Benedicite* (Song of the Three) is sung in the Liturgy of the Hours every Sunday.

## In Scripture

- The Book of Daniel (entire — 12 chapters Hebrew, 14 chapters Catholic)
- Matthew 24:15 (Christ refers to Daniel directly)
- Matthew 26:64 (Christ uses Daniel's "Son of Man" before the Sanhedrin)
- Revelation 13 (the beasts), 20 (the books opened)
