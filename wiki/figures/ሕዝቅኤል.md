# ነቢዩ ሕዝቅኤል — The Prophet Ezekiel

**Type:** figure
**Amharic:** ነቢዩ ሕዝቅኤል
**Variants:** Yechezkel (יְחֶזְקֵאל — "God strengthens")
**English:** Ezekiel
**Role:** Major prophet of the Babylonian exile, prophet of the Spirit and the new heart
**Traditional dates:** c. 622–570 BC
**Feast day:** April 10 (መጋቢት 27) in some traditions
**Sources:** 4
**Last updated:** 2026-05-09

## Biography

**ሕዝቅኤል (Ezekiel)** was a **priest** carried into Babylonian exile in the first deportation (597 BC). He prophesied to the exile community by the river **Chebar** in Babylon for some 22 years (c. 593–571 BC) — overlapping with Jeremiah's ministry in Jerusalem and Daniel's at the Babylonian court.

His ministry is shaped by **dramatic visions and symbolic actions**: God commands him to lie on his side for 390 days, to eat bread baked over dung, to refrain from mourning his wife — every action a sermon to a stunned exile community.

He is called *"son of man"* by God 93 times — emphasizing his frail humanity before the divine mystery. The same title Christ would adopt for himself.

## The four great visions

### 1. The Chariot Throne (ሕዝ. 1)

The opening vision — four living creatures (lion, ox, eagle, man) supporting the throne of God. These four became the **symbols of the four Evangelists** in Christian iconography:

- **Lion** — Mark
- **Ox** — Luke
- **Eagle** — John
- **Man** — Matthew

Ezekiel sees God *"like the appearance of fire"* enthroned above the cherubim — and falls on his face.

### 2. The temple's glory departing (ሕዝ. 8–11)

Ezekiel sees in vision the **glory of the Lord lifting from the Temple in Jerusalem** and departing — a sign that the Temple was about to be destroyed (which it was in 587 BC). God leaves the place that has become unfaithful.

### 3. The valley of dry bones (ሕዝ. 37)

The most famous Ezekiel passage. God leads him to a valley filled with dry bones and asks: *"Son of man, can these bones live?"*

> «"መተንፈስ — በትንፋሽ ላይ ና — ሰማይ ላይ — ትንፋሽ ብለሽ ተናገሪ — በእነዚህ ቤዛዎች ላይ ተንፍሺ ብለሽ።"»
> — ሕዝ. 37:9

Bones come together. Sinews. Flesh. Skin. Then breath — the Spirit — and they live, *"a great army."* This is **the prophecy of bodily resurrection** the Catholic Church reads at the Easter Vigil. The dry bones are Israel in exile — but ultimately, every person who has ever died.

### 4. The new temple and the river (ሕዝ. 40–48)

The closing vision: a **new temple** of perfect proportions, and from beneath its altar a **river** flowing east, getting deeper and deeper, reaching the sea — making it sweet — bringing every kind of fruit tree to bear leaves for the healing of the nations.

The vision is fulfilled in:
- **The Church** as the new Temple where God dwells.
- **The water from Christ's side** on the cross (ዮሐ. 19:34).
- **The river of life** in the heavenly Jerusalem (ራእይ 22:1–2).

## The new heart and the Spirit

Ezekiel's deepest theological contribution is the promise of a **new heart and a new spirit**:

> «አዲስ ልብ እሰጣችኋለሁ — አዲስ መንፈስ በውስጣችሁ አኖራለሁ።
> የድንጋዩን ልብ ከሥጋችሁ አስወጣለሁ — የሥጋ ልብ እሰጣችኋለሁ።
> መንፈሴን በውስጣችሁ አኖራለሁ — በሥርዓቴ እንድትሄዱ አደርጋችኋለሁ።»
> — ሕዝ. 36:26–27

This is the Old Testament's clearest promise of the **interior gift of the Holy Spirit** — fulfilled at Pentecost (የሐዋ. ሥራ 2) and in every Christian baptism. Ezekiel saw what Pentecost would be five centuries before it happened.

## In the Liturgy

- Ezekiel 36:26–27 is read at every adult baptism — the prophecy of the new heart fulfilled in the catechumen.
- Ezekiel 37 (the dry bones) is one of the readings at the **Easter Vigil**.
- Ezekiel 1 (the chariot vision) shapes Christian iconography of the Four Evangelists.

## In Scripture

- The Book of Ezekiel (entire — 48 chapters)
- New Testament echoes: Christ as "Son of man" (a self-designation drawn from Ezekiel)
- Revelation 4 (the four living creatures)
- Revelation 22 (the river of life)
