## Biography

**ቅዱስ ዮሐንስ (Saint John)** was the younger son of Zebedee, brother of **James the Greater**, and a Galilean fisherman. He left his nets at Christ's first call (ማቴ. 4:21–22) and remained with him to the end. Tradition identifies him as the **"beloved disciple"** repeatedly mentioned in the Fourth Gospel (ዮሐ. 13:23, 19:26, 20:2, 21:7, 21:20).

He was the **only apostle** at the foot of the Cross. From there Christ entrusted his Mother to John's care:

> «ሴት ሆይ — ይኸው ልጅሽ … ይኸው እናትህ።»
> — ዮሐ. 19:26–27

From that hour, John took Mary into his own house. He became the spiritual son of the Mother of God — and through him, every disciple is.

## After Pentecost

John worked closely with Peter (የሐዋ. ሥራ 3:1, 4:13, 8:14). He wrote four New Testament books:

- **The Gospel of John** — the most theological of the four Gospels
- **1, 2, 3 John** — the three Catholic epistles
- **The Revelation** — written from exile on the island of **Patmos** under Emperor Domitian (c. 95 AD)

He is traditionally identified with the **theology of the Word (Logos)** — *"In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with God, and the Word was God"* (ዮሐ. 1:1).

## Death

John is the **only apostle not martyred** by violent death. Tradition records that he was thrown into boiling oil at Rome but emerged unharmed (the Latin Church commemorates this on May 6). He died of old age at **Ephesus** around 100 AD — the **last living apostle** and the last eyewitness of Christ.
