## What they are

The **Beatitudes** (Latin *beatitudo* = "blessedness") are the opening of Christ's **Sermon on the Mount** — the constitution of the Kingdom of Heaven and the moral heart of the Gospel. They name the eight kinds of person whom God calls **blessed (ብፁዕ)**.

> «ኢየሱስ … ይህን አስተምራቸው ጀመር።»
> — ማቴ. 5:1–2

The Beatitudes overturn worldly judgment: those the world calls miserable — the poor, the sorrowful, the hungry, the persecuted — Christ calls **blessed**. The Compendium (Q360) teaches that the Beatitudes reveal the very face of Christ himself: each one is a portrait of the Master before being a precept for the disciple.

## The Eight Beatitudes (ማቴዎስ 5:3–10)

### 1. Blessed are the poor in spirit
> «በመንፈስ ድሆች የሆኑ ብፁዓን ናቸው — መንግሥተ ሰማያት የእነርሱ ናትና።»

**Amharic:** በመንፈስ ድሆች የሆኑ ብፁዓን ናቸው።

**Meaning:** Not those who have no money, but those who do not cling to their possessions or self-importance. The empty hand can receive God; the full hand cannot. The Compendium's teaching on detachment finds its root here.

### 2. Blessed are those who mourn
> «የሚያዝኑ ብፁዓን ናቸው — እነርሱ ይጽናናሉና።»

**Amharic:** የሚያዝኑ ብፁዓን ናቸው።

**Meaning:** Those who weep over their own sins, the world's sins, and the sufferings of others. Mourning is the soul's true response to a broken world. God will himself wipe away their tears (ራእይ 21:4).

### 3. Blessed are the meek
> «የዋሆች ብፁዓን ናቸው — እነርሱ ምድርን ይወርሳሉና።»

**Amharic:** የዋሆች ብፁዓን ናቸው።

**Meaning:** The gentle, those who refuse violence and retaliation. Strength under control. Christ called himself *meek and humble of heart* (ማቴ. 11:29). The meek do not seize the earth — they inherit it.

### 4. Blessed are those who hunger and thirst for righteousness
> «ስለ ጽድቅ የሚራቡና የሚጠሙ ብፁዓን ናቸው — እነርሱ ይጠግባሉና።»

**Amharic:** ስለ ጽድቅ የሚራቡና የሚጠሙ ብፁዓን ናቸው።

**Meaning:** Those who long for what God wants more than for what they want — for justice in society, for holiness in self, for truth in all things. God will satisfy this hunger himself (ማቴ. 5:6).

### 5. Blessed are the merciful
> «መሐሪዎች ብፁዓን ናቸው — እነርሱ ምሕረትን ያገኛሉና።»

**Amharic:** መሐሪዎች ብፁዓን ናቸው።

**Meaning:** Those who treat others with the mercy they themselves need. The works of mercy (see [[teaching/works-of-mercy]]) are the visible shape of this beatitude. The condition is iron: *"As we forgive those who trespass against us"* — the Lord's Prayer makes our forgiveness conditional on our forgiving.

### 6. Blessed are the pure in heart
> «ልባቸው ንጹሕ የሆነ ብፁዓን ናቸው — እነርሱ እግዚአብሔርን ያያሉና።»

**Amharic:** ልባቸው ንጹሕ የሆነ ብፁዓን ናቸው።

**Meaning:** Those whose interior is undivided — single-eyed, not torn between God and idols. Sexual purity is part, but only part. The deeper purity is the **single love** that makes all of life one.

### 7. Blessed are the peacemakers
> «ሰላም የሚያደርጉ ብፁዓን ናቸው — እነርሱ የእግዚአብሔር ልጆች ይባላሉና።»

**Amharic:** ሰላም የሚያደርጉ ብፁዓን ናቸው።

**Meaning:** Peace is not absence of conflict — it is the *tranquility of order* (St. Augustine, *City of God* XIX.13). Peacemakers reconcile enemies, heal divisions, refuse gossip and faction. They share the work of Christ — who reconciled God and humanity in his own body.

### 8. Blessed are those persecuted for righteousness' sake
> «ስለ ጽድቅ የሚሰደዱ ብፁዓን ናቸው — መንግሥተ ሰማያት የእነርሱ ናትና።»

**Amharic:** ስለ ጽድቅ የሚሰደዱ ብፁዓን ናቸው።

**Meaning:** The Beatitudes turn full circle. The first and the last name the same reward — *the Kingdom of Heaven*. The first beatitude (poverty of spirit) is the disposition; the eighth (persecution) is the climax. The disciple who has truly become poor, mournful, meek, hungry for righteousness, merciful, pure, and peacemaking will inevitably encounter opposition. **Persecution is the seal of authenticity.**

## A ninth beatitude — for the disciples themselves

> «ብፁዓን ናችሁ — ሰዎች ስለ እኔ ሲወቅሱአችሁና ሲያሳድዱአችሁ … በሰማይ ብዙ ዋጋ አላችሁና።»
> — ማቴ. 5:11–12

Christ adds, in personal address: *"Blessed are you when men revile you for my sake."* The Beatitudes are not abstract teaching — they are an **invitation** that the disciple either accepts or refuses.

## The Lukan parallel — and the woes

Saint Luke's Sermon on the Plain (ሉቃ. 6:20–26) gives a shorter list — four beatitudes, four corresponding **woes**:

| Blessed are… | Woe to… |
|---|---|
| The poor | The rich |
| The hungry | The full |
| Those who weep | Those who laugh |
| Those persecuted | Those everyone speaks well of |

Matthew spiritualizes (poor *in spirit*); Luke leaves the social meaning sharp. **Both are true.** The Catholic tradition holds them together: the spiritual reading does not erase the social one. The poor of the world have a privileged place in the heart of Christ.

## How to live the Beatitudes

Saint Augustine famously read the Beatitudes as a **ladder of ascent** — each one builds on the previous:

1. **Poverty of spirit** opens the soul to receive.
2. **Mourning** softens the soul to be moved.
3. **Meekness** disposes the soul to receive instruction.
4. **Hunger for righteousness** drives the soul to seek truth.
5. **Mercy** turns the soul outward to others.
6. **Purity of heart** clarifies the soul's vision of God.
7. **Peacemaking** makes the soul a co-worker with Christ.
8. **Persecution** completes and seals the soul's transformation.

This is the entire spiritual life in eight verses.

## Christ as the Beatitudes

Read the Gospels and you find that **Jesus himself** is the perfect fulfillment of every Beatitude:

- He had nowhere to lay his head (Matt. 8:20) — *poor in spirit*.
- He wept over Jerusalem and at Lazarus's tomb — *mourning*.
- He was meek and humble of heart — *meekness*.
- He hungered to do the Father's will (John 4:34) — *hunger for righteousness*.
- He showed mercy to sinners, the sick, the outcast — *merciful*.
- His heart was utterly undivided — *pure in heart*.
- He reconciled humanity to God on the cross — *peacemaker*.
- He was persecuted unto death — *persecuted for righteousness*.

To live the Beatitudes is, in the end, to **become Christlike**. They are his self-portrait, and they are the path to becoming his image.
