## What they are

The **capital sins** are not the *worst* sins one can commit. They are called *capital* (from Latin *caput*, "head") because they are the **heads** or **roots** from which countless other sins spring. Conquer the capital, and you cut off a whole family of sins.

The Compendium teaches (Q398) that the seven capital sins each have an **opposing virtue** — the daily medicine for the daily disease.

## How each sin works

### 1. ትዕቢት (Pride)

The root of all sin. The desire to be the standard of one's own life — *to be like God* (ዘፍ. 3:5). Every other sin grows from putting *self* before *God* and *neighbor*. Lucifer's sin. Adam's sin. The first sin always.

> «ትዕቢት ጥፋትን ያመጣል — ኩሩ መንፈስም ውድቀትን።»
> — ምሳ. 16:18

**Antidote:** ትሕትና — truthful self-knowledge. The saints are not those who think they are nothing; they are those who know exactly what they are before God, and forget themselves in love.

### 2. ስስት (Greed)

Inordinate love of wealth and possessions. Money is good; the *love* of money is the root of all evils (1ኛ ጢሞ. 6:10). Greed strangles the soul, hardens the heart, and binds the conscience.

**Antidote:** ልግሥና — generosity. Tithe. Give in secret. Treat your wealth as God's, you as steward.

### 3. ዝሙት (Lust)

Disordered sexual desire. Sex is a gift, ordered to faithful marriage and the gift of life. Lust uses the body of another for pleasure without the love that justifies the act.

**Antidote:** ንጽሕና — chastity in the state of life God has called you to (single, married, religious). Custody of the eyes. Avoid occasions of sin.

### 4. ምቀኝነት (Envy)

Sorrow at another's good. Envy is *not* the desire for something good — it is the desire that *the other* not have it. Cain's sin against Abel. The Pharisees' sin against Christ.

**Antidote:** Rejoice in the success of others. Pray for them by name. *"Rejoice with those who rejoice"* (ሮሜ 12:15).

### 5. ስግብግብነት (Gluttony)

Disordered desire for food and drink. Includes drunkenness, addiction to substances, eating to fill emotional emptiness rather than the body's need.

**Antidote:** ጾም — fasting. The Church's fasting days (Wednesdays, Fridays, Lent, Advent) are not arbitrary punishments; they are the medicine of the body that frees the soul.

### 6. ቁጣ (Wrath)

Disordered anger — not the righteous anger Christ showed at the Temple, but anger that nurses grudges, plots revenge, or destroys peace.

**Antidote:** ትዕግሥት — patience. *"Be slow to anger"* (ያዕቆብ 1:19). Forgive quickly. Offer the day's irritations to God.

### 7. ስንፍና (Sloth)

Not laziness in worldly things but **spiritual sloth** — coldness toward God, neglect of prayer, abandonment of the duties of the spiritual life. The saints called this the **noonday devil** because it strikes hardest in middle life.

**Antidote:** ትጋት — fervor. Keep the rule of life. Get up for Mass when you don't feel like it. Show up to prayer when prayer is dry.

## How to use this list

- **In examination of conscience.** Walk through the seven before confession.
- **In daily examen.** At evening, ask: which one weighed on me today?
- **For habit-breaking.** Pick the one that troubles you most. Practice the opposing virtue deliberately for a month.
- **For the family.** Teach children the names — give them words for what they feel.

## The deeper diagnosis

Each capital sin is a **good thing distorted**. Pride is dignity gone wrong. Greed is providence gone wrong. Lust is love gone wrong. The remedy is never to despise the good — it is to **order** it, to put each created thing in its proper place under God.

> «እግዚአብሔር ሆይ — ነፍሴን አንተ ነህ።»
> — መዝ. 73:25
