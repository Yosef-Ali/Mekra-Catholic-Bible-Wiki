# How to Receive Holy Communion (ቅዱስ ቁርባንን እንዴት መቀበል)

**Type:** practical
**Amharic:** ቅዱስ ቁርባንን መቀበል
**Compendium Q:** 290–294
**CCC:** 1384–1401
**Sources:** 4
**Last updated:** 2026-05-08
**Related:** [[teaching/eucharist]], [[teaching/practical-mass]], [[teaching/practical-confession]], [[concepts/ቅዱስ-ቁርባን]], [[apologetics/eucharist-real-presence]]

## What you are receiving (የምትቀበለው)

You are not receiving bread and wine — you are receiving **Jesus Christ himself**, body, blood, soul, and divinity, under the appearances of bread and wine. The Compendium teaches (Q282): *"በቅዱስ ቁርባን ምሥጢር ኢየሱስ ክርስቶስ ራሱ — በሙሉ — በውስጡ ይገኛል፤ ሥጋውና ደሙ፣ ነፍሱና መለኮቱ።"*

This is why every aspect of receiving Communion must be done with reverence.

## Three conditions to receive (ሦስቱ መሥፈርቶች)

You may receive Communion only if **all three** are true:

1. **You are a baptized Catholic in full communion with the Church.**
2. **You are in the state of grace** — not conscious of any unconfessed mortal sin. If you have committed mortal sin, you must go to confession first.
3. **You have observed the Eucharistic fast** — at least one hour without food or drink (water and medicine excepted) before receiving.

If any of these is missing, do not receive. You may still come forward with arms crossed over your chest to receive a blessing.

## Before approaching the altar (ወደ መሠዊያ ከመቅረብ በፊት)

- Recall who you are about to receive.
- Make an act of contrition for venial sins.
- Pray quietly: *"Lord, I am not worthy that you should enter under my roof, but only say the word and my soul shall be healed."*
- Approach with reverence — eyes lowered or fixed on the host.

## Receiving on the tongue (በምላስ መቀበል)

The traditional Ethiopian Catholic and universal Catholic manner.

1. Open your mouth and extend your tongue slightly.
2. The priest places the host on your tongue.
3. Close your mouth, consume immediately, and return to your place.

## Receiving in the hand (በእጅ መቀበል)

Where permitted by the local bishop:

1. Place one hand flat under the other, forming a "throne" for the King. (If you are right-handed, left hand on top; if left-handed, the reverse.)
2. The priest places the host on your upper hand.
3. Step aside, take the host with your other hand, and consume immediately **before stepping away from the priest**. Never carry the host away.
4. Return to your place.

## Receiving the Precious Blood (ደሙን መቀበል)

If the cup is offered:

1. Approach.
2. Take the cup gently with both hands.
3. Drink a small sip.
4. Return the cup carefully to the minister.

You receive **Christ entire** in either the host or the cup. Receiving under both kinds is a fuller sign but is not required.

## After receiving (ከተቀበልክ በኋላ)

Return quietly to your place. **Kneel** and pray. This is the most precious moment of your day — Christ is dwelling in you sacramentally for about 15 minutes (until the appearances of bread are dissolved).

### A simple prayer after Communion

> «ጌታዬ ኢየሱስ፣ በውስጤ የመጣህ አመሰግንሃለሁ።
> ልቤን አንተ ብቻ ግዛ።
> ቃልህን አስታውሰኝ፤ ፈቃድህን አስኪደኝ፤
> ለሌሎች የክርስቶስ መሆን አስችለኝ።
> ፍቅርህ በውስጤ ይኑር፤ ለዘላለሙ።
> አሜን።»

## Mortal sin and Communion (ገዳይ ኃጢአትና ቁርባን)

Saint Paul warns sternly:

> «ይህን እንጀራ የበላ ወይም የጌታን ጽዋ ያለ ብቁነት የጠጣ የጌታን ሥጋና ደም በደለኛ ይሆናል። ስለዚህ ሰው ራሱን ይመርምር፤ እንደዚሁም ከእንጀራው ይብላ፤ ከጽዋውም ይጠጣ።»
> — 1ኛ ቆሮንቶስ 11:27–28

Receiving Communion in mortal sin is a **further sin** — the sin of sacrilege. **Always confess first.** If a mortal sin is suddenly remembered after entering the Communion line, return to your seat without going forward — no embarrassment is greater than the sin of sacrilege.

## How often should I receive?

- **Required:** at least once a year, in the Easter season ("Easter duty").
- **Recommended:** every Sunday, the Lord's day.
- **Permissible:** daily, if attending daily Mass.
- **Limit:** twice in one day, and only if the second reception is at a full Mass you attend.

## Special situations

- **First Communion** — children typically receive after age 7, having been catechized to know what they are receiving.
- **For the sick or homebound** — a priest, deacon, or extraordinary minister can bring Communion to the home or hospital. Ask your parish.
- **Viaticum** — the Eucharist given to the dying as "food for the journey" to eternal life. Always call a priest when a Catholic is gravely ill.
