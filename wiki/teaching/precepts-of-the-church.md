## What they are

The **Precepts of the Church** are the **bare minimum** required of a Catholic — the floor below which a Catholic cannot fall and remain in good standing with the Church. They are not the *whole* Christian life, only the **non-negotiable basics** that guarantee a soul receives at least the necessary spiritual care.

The Compendium teaches (Q432) that these precepts exist to **support and protect** the Christian life — not to burden it.

## The Six Precepts

The Catholic tradition lists **five or six** precepts depending on the diocesan formulation. The standard list:

### 1. Attend Mass on Sundays and holy days of obligation

**ቅዳሴን በበዓላት እና በሰንበት ላይ መካፈል።**

The most important precept. Sunday is **the Lord's Day** — the day of the Resurrection. To skip Mass deliberately and without serious cause is **mortal sin** (CCC 2181). Holy days of obligation in the Ethiopian Catholic calendar (vary by diocese, generally include):

- Christmas (ገና)
- Epiphany / Timket (ጥምቀት)
- Easter (ፋሲካ)
- Ascension (ዕርገት)
- Pentecost (ጴራቅሊጦስ)
- Assumption (ፍልሰታ)
- All Saints (የቅዱሳን ሁሉ ቀን)
- Immaculate Conception
- Patronal feasts of one's parish or diocese

### 2. Confess sins at least once a year

**ቢያንስ በዓመት አንድ ጊዜ መናዘዝ።**

The minimum, though monthly is recommended. Required *especially* before receiving Easter Communion. Anyone conscious of mortal sin **must** confess before receiving the Eucharist. See [[teaching/practical-confession]].

### 3. Receive the Eucharist at least during the Easter season

**ቢያንስ በፋሲካ ወቅት ቅዱስ ቁርባንን መቀበል። (ፋሲካዊ ዕዳ)**

Called the **"Easter duty."** A baptized Catholic who has reached the age of reason must receive Holy Communion at least once a year, ideally during the Paschal season (Lent through Pentecost). One who refuses for years to receive the Eucharist places himself outside the visible communion of the Church.

### 4. Observe the days of fasting and abstinence

**የጾምንና የመከልከልን ቀኖች መጠበቅ።**

The Catholic Church requires:
- **Abstinence from meat** on Fridays of Lent (and traditionally all Fridays of the year, though some bishops permit substitute penance).
- **Fasting** (one full meal + two smaller, no meat) on **Ash Wednesday** and **Good Friday (ስቅለት)**.
- The Eucharistic fast — **one hour** without food or drink (water and medicine excepted) before receiving Communion.

The Ethiopian Catholic Church inherits a much **richer fasting tradition** from the Tewahedo heritage — including Wednesday and Friday weekly fasts and the seasonal fasts of Advent (የነቢያት ጾም), Lent (ሁዳዴ ጾም), the Apostles (የሐዋርያት ጾም), and the Assumption (ፍልሰታ ጾም). These additional fasts go beyond the universal precept but are deeply appropriate to Ethiopian spiritual life.

### 5. Provide for the material needs of the Church

**ቤተክርስቲያንን በቁሳዊ ድጋፍ መርዳት።**

Each according to ability — through tithing, regular giving, support of clergy, support of the parish's outreach to the poor. The clergy live by the offerings of the people; the works of mercy live by the same.

### 6. Observe the laws of the Church concerning marriage

**የቤተክርስቲያን የጋብቻ ሕግ መጠበቅ።**

A Catholic marriage requires the Catholic form (before a priest and two witnesses) for validity unless dispensed. Marrying outside the Church is invalid. See [[teaching/practical-marriage]].

## Why precepts at all?

Some Christians — Pentecostal and evangelical — say *"I follow Christ, not laws."* The Catholic answer:

1. **Christ himself gave commandments** (ዮሐ. 14:15: "If you love me, keep my commandments").
2. **The Apostles bound the early Church** with concrete decisions (የሐዋ. ሥራ 15:28–29: the Council of Jerusalem).
3. **The Church's authority to bind and loose** comes from Christ (ማቴ. 16:19, 18:18).

A precept is a **safety floor** — not a ceiling. The saints far surpassed the precepts. But the precepts ensure that even a struggling Catholic, doing the bare minimum, receives at least the sacraments necessary for salvation.

## Mortal vs. venial breach

Most precepts, broken deliberately and without serious cause, are **mortal sin** in their core matter:

- **Skipping Sunday Mass** through one's own fault (not illness, not unavoidable obligation).
- **Refusing to confess** for years.
- **Refusing the Easter Communion** for years.
- **Marrying outside the Church** while baptized Catholic.

Lesser failures (forgetting a Friday abstinence, modest under-giving) are usually venial — confessable but not damning.

## What the precepts are *not*

- ❌ A complete Christian life.
- ❌ A way to "earn" heaven.
- ❌ A merit system.
- ❌ A burden imposed by clergy beyond Scripture.

They are the **floor.** A house with only a floor is no house. But a house without a floor collapses. The precepts hold the floor.

## How to live above the floor

The precepts say *Mass on Sunday*. The saint goes to Mass daily.
The precepts say *confess once a year*. The saint confesses monthly.
The precepts say *one Easter Communion*. The saint communicates frequently.
The precepts say *abstain from meat on Fridays*. The saint fasts on Wednesday too.
The precepts say *support the Church*. The saint tithes and gives the widow's mite besides.
The precepts say *marry properly*. The saint makes the marriage a domestic church.

The precepts give the shape; love fills the volume.
