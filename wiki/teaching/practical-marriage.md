## Synthesis

ጋብቻ የእግዚአብሔር ሥራ ነው — በፍጥረት መጀመሪያ የተመሠረተ፤ በክርስቶስ ወደ ምሥጢርነት የተነሣ። የተጠመቀ ወንድና ሴት የሚገቡት ጋብቻ የክርስቶስና የቤተክርስቲያን ፍቅር ምሳሌ ነው። የምሥጢሩ ሦስት ምልክቶች **ነጻ ፈቃድ፣ ዘላለማዊ ታማኝነት፣ ለልጆች ክፍት መሆን** ናቸው። ጋብቻ ሊፈርስ የማይችል ነው — *"እግዚአብሔር ያጣመረውን ሰው አይለየው"* (ማቴ. 19:6)።

## Three essential properties (ሦስቱ ዋና ባሕርያት)

A Catholic marriage must have:

1. **Unity** — one man and one woman, exclusively, for life.
2. **Indissolubility** — until death parts them. No civil divorce can dissolve a sacramental marriage.
3. **Openness to children** — accepting children lovingly from God; no contraceptive intent.

If any of these is rejected at the moment of consent, the marriage is invalid.

## Who can marry in the Catholic Church?

- **Both baptized:** the marriage is a **sacrament**.
- **One Catholic, one baptized non-Catholic** (e.g., Orthodox, Protestant): a sacramental "mixed marriage" — needs permission from the bishop. Requires the Catholic party to promise to do all in their power to raise children Catholic.
- **One Catholic, one unbaptized**: a "disparity of cult" marriage — needs a *dispensation* (not just permission). The marriage is natural, not sacramental, but valid.
- **Two unbaptized:** a natural marriage, indissoluble but not sacramental.

## Impediments to marriage

The Church recognizes 12 *diriment impediments* — conditions that make marriage impossible. The most common:

- Existing prior valid marriage of either party (no annulment yet).
- Holy orders or perpetual religious vows.
- Close blood relationship (consanguinity) up to fourth degree.
- Affinity (in-law relationship) in the direct line.
- Age — under 16 (man) or 14 (woman) without dispensation.
- Force or grave fear coercing consent.
- Inability to consummate (impotence, not infertility).

If any impediment exists, see a priest before any wedding planning. Many impediments can be dispensed; some cannot.

## Marriage preparation (Pre-Cana)

Most Ethiopian Catholic dioceses require a 3–6 month preparation. Topics covered:

1. **Theology of marriage** — sacrament, covenant, indissolubility, openness to children.
2. **Communication and conflict** — practical skills for a healthy marriage.
3. **Financial planning** — joint finances as stewardship.
4. **Natural Family Planning (NFP)** — methods for spacing children in conformity with Church teaching.
5. **Mixed marriage / disparity of cult** — special preparation if applicable.
6. **Wedding liturgy planning** — readings, music, vows.

You will also complete:
- A **pre-marital inquiry** with the parish priest.
- Recent **baptismal certificates** (issued within 6 months for the marriage).
- **Confirmation certificates**.
- **Free-state declarations** (proving you are not currently married).
- **Witnesses' affidavits** if required.

## The wedding liturgy

A Catholic wedding takes place during a **Mass** when both parties are Catholic, or at a Liturgy of the Word for mixed marriages.

### Order of the rite

1. **Entrance and greeting**
2. **Liturgy of the Word** — usually three readings; the bride and groom often choose.
3. **Homily**
4. **The Rite of Marriage:**
   - Questions about freedom, faithfulness, openness to children.
   - **Exchange of consent** — the heart of the sacrament.
   - **Blessing and exchange of rings.**
5. **Liturgy of the Eucharist** (in a Mass) or final blessing.
6. **Nuptial blessing** — the priest's special blessing on the couple.
7. **Final blessing** of the assembly.

### The vow (ቃለ ኪዳን)

> «እኔ (ስም)፣ አንተ (ስም)ን ሚስቴ/ባለቤቴ አድርጌ እወስዳለሁ።
> ከዛሬ ጀምሮ፣ በደስታም በመከራም፣ በብልጽግናም በድህነትም፣ በጤናም በሕመምም፣
> ሞት እስኪለየን ድረስ እወድሃለሁ፣ አከብርሃለሁ።
> ለፍቅሬና ለታማኝነቴ የእግዚአብሔርን ቃል እምላለሁ።»

The exchange of consent — freely, mutually, before witnesses — is the sacrament. The priest does not "marry" the couple; the spouses confer the sacrament on each other. The priest is the Church's witness.

## After the wedding — the lifelong work

Marriage is **not** a destination. It is the beginning of a vocation.

### Pillars of a Catholic marriage

- **Sunday Mass together.** Worship as a family.
- **Daily prayer together.** Even five minutes — a shared rosary, an evening prayer.
- **Frequent confession.** Forgive each other quickly; let no day end in anger.
- **Tithe and almsgiving together.** Money reflects priorities.
- **Children received as gifts.** Use Natural Family Planning if needed; never contraception.
- **Marriage enrichment.** Annual retreat, marriage encounter, ongoing formation.
- **The domestic church.** Your home is the first Church the children will know.

### When difficulties come

- Pray together first.
- Speak with a Catholic priest, deacon, or trained Catholic counselor.
- Civil separation may be permitted in cases of grave harm. Civil divorce does not end the marriage in God's eyes.
- The Church's **annulment process** examines whether a valid marriage existed in the first place — it is not "Catholic divorce."

## Common questions

**Can a divorced Catholic remarry in the Church?**
Only if the prior marriage has been declared null by a Church tribunal. A civil divorce does not free a person to remarry sacramentally.

**Can two non-Catholics convert and "redo" their marriage?**
If they were both baptized at the time of their wedding, their marriage was already a sacrament. They simply continue. If only one was baptized, a *radical sanation* may be requested.

**Is contraception really a serious sin?**
The Church teaches it is gravely contrary to the openness to life that defines marriage (Compendium Q499; *Humanae Vitae* §11–14). Natural Family Planning is permitted for serious reasons.
