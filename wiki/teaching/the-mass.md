## What the Mass IS

ቅዳሴ — **the Mass** — is **the central act of Catholic worship and the source and summit of the entire Christian life** (Compendium Q274; CCC 1324). It is at once:

1. **The making-present of Christ's one sacrifice on Calvary** — not a new sacrifice, but the same one re-presented in time.
2. **The memorial of the Last Supper** — Christ taking, blessing, breaking, and giving.
3. **The communion in the Body and Blood of Christ** — sacramental reception under the appearances of bread and wine.
4. **The pledge of the heavenly banquet** — anticipation of the Wedding Feast of the Lamb.
5. **The fullest prayer of the Church** — the whole Body of Christ on earth uniting with the Body in heaven and the Body being purified.

The Compendium (Q280): *"ቁርባን — የክርስቶስን አንድ መሥዋዕት — በምሥጢራዊ ሁኔታ ያስታውሳል — ያውነታዊ ያደርጋል።"*

## What the Mass is NOT

- ❌ A symbol — Christ is **really, truly, substantially present** (Q282).
- ❌ A new sacrifice — Christ died **once for all** (ዕብ. 9:28). The Mass re-presents that one offering, not a new one.
- ❌ A community meal alone — communal it is, but rooted in **sacrifice**.
- ❌ The priest's private act — the priest acts *in persona Christi* and *in persona ecclesiae*; the people's participation is essential.
- ❌ A performance — Mass is a *liturgy*, the work of God before the work of man.

## The Two Tables of the Mass

### 1. The Table of the Word (የቃሉ ማዕድ)

We are fed first at the **table of God's word**. Scripture proclaimed at Mass is not just *read about* God — Christ himself speaks (CCC 1088).

Components:
- **Entrance and Penitential Act** — preparation
- **Gloria** (omitted in Lent and Advent)
- **Collect** — the gathering prayer
- **Old Testament reading**
- **Responsorial Psalm**
- **New Testament reading** (Epistle or Acts)
- **Gospel Acclamation** (Alleluia, except in Lent)
- **Gospel** — the high point of the Word
- **Homily** — the priest applying the Word to the assembly's life
- **Creed** — the assembly's response of faith
- **Prayers of the Faithful** — intercessions

### 2. The Table of the Eucharist (የቅዱስ ቁርባን ማዕድ)

We are fed second at the **table of Christ's body and blood**.

Components:
- **Offertory** — bread and wine brought forward, representing the people's lives
- **Preface** — opening of the great prayer
- **Sanctus** — *"Holy, Holy, Holy"* — joining the angels' hymn
- **Eucharistic Prayer** — the great central prayer of the Mass:
  - *Epiclesis* — invoking the Holy Spirit upon the gifts
  - **Consecration** — *"This is my body… this is my blood"* — the bread and wine become Christ's Body and Blood (transubstantiation)
  - *Anamnesis* — the memorial of the saving deeds
  - *Intercession* — for the Church on earth, the dead, and oneself
- **Doxology and Great Amen** — the assembly seals the prayer
- **Our Father** — preparing for communion
- **Sign of Peace** — reconciliation before the altar
- **Lamb of God** — *"Agnus Dei"*
- **Communion** — reception of the Body and Blood
- **Prayer after Communion** — thanksgiving
- **Final Blessing and Dismissal** — *"Ite missa est"* — sent into the world

For the practical "what to do at each part," see [[teaching/practical-mass]].

## The Sacrifice — what makes the Mass the Mass

The Council of Trent (1562) defined that the Mass is a **true and proper sacrifice** — not because Christ is "killed again" (he cannot die again), but because **the same Christ who offered himself on Calvary makes that offering present** under the sacramental signs.

Five elements of the **one sacrifice** are present:

1. **The same Priest** — Christ, acting through the ordained priest as his instrument.
2. **The same Victim** — Christ himself, given under the appearances of bread and wine.
3. **The same Offering** — to the Father, in the Holy Spirit.
4. **The same Purpose** — for the salvation of the world, for the living and the dead.
5. **A different mode** — bloodily on Calvary; sacramentally and unbloodily at every Mass.

> «ይህን — ለእኔ መታሰቢያ — አድርጉ።»
> — ሉቃ. 22:19

The word *"do this"* (ποιεῖτε) had a sacrificial meaning in Greek of the era. Christ commanded the apostles to **continue the sacrificial offering** — and this is the priestly act of every Catholic Eucharist since.

## The Mass and Heaven

The Book of Revelation describes the **heavenly liturgy**: the throne of God, the slain Lamb, the elders, the angels, the incense of the saints' prayers, the four living creatures, the cry *"Holy, Holy, Holy"* (ራእይ 4–5).

This is **the same liturgy** the Church on earth celebrates. When the priest leads the Sanctus, the assembly is joining the choir of angels around the heavenly throne. When the priest elevates the Host, the saints in heaven adore the same Christ.

> «ኢየሩሳሌም ሰማያዊ — ብዙ ሺ መላእክት — የመጀመሪያዎቹ ሕዝብ … ስብሰባ ነው።»
> — ዕብ. 12:22–23

The Mass is heaven invading earth. Not metaphor — sacramental reality.

## Why daily Mass?

The Sunday obligation is the **floor**. But many Catholics attend daily Mass because:

- It is the **most powerful prayer** that exists.
- It draws **specific graces** for that day's challenges.
- It nurtures **intimate friendship** with Christ in the Eucharist.
- It offers **petitions** for living and dead with the strongest spiritual weight any prayer can carry.

Pope John Paul II spoke from a lifetime of daily Mass: *"How could one ever do without it?"*

## The Ge'ez Rite vs. the Latin Rite

Ethiopian Catholic Christians belong to one of two rites:

- **The Ge'ez Catholic Rite** — celebrating the same essential Mass in the **ancient Ethiopian liturgical tradition**, in Ge'ez and Amharic. Shares much with the Ethiopian Tewahedo Qiddase but in full communion with Rome. Includes specific Ethiopian anaphoras (Eucharistic prayers) — Twelve Apostles, Our Lord, John Chrysostom, etc.
- **The Latin Rite** — celebrating the Mass in the Roman tradition (commonly in Amharic in Ethiopia, but historically in Latin).

Both are **the same Mass** — same essence, same Christ, same sacrifice. The differences are in liturgical language and ritual style, not in substance.

The Catholic Church recognizes both as fully valid and equally precious. A Catholic may attend either and fulfill the Sunday obligation.

## Common errors corrected

**"It's just a meal."** — No. It is a sacrifice in the form of a meal. The Last Supper itself was a *Passover meal* — and the Passover was a sacrificial meal. Christ deliberately chose this form.

**"The bread is just a symbol."** — No. The Catholic Church has taught the **real presence** without break for 2,000 years. Christ's words *"This is my body"* are taken at face value — as the early Church Fathers taught (Justin Martyr, Ignatius of Antioch, Cyril of Jerusalem).

**"Anyone can preside."** — No. Only a validly ordained bishop or priest in apostolic succession can confect the Eucharist. Without him, no Mass.

**"It's the same in every Christian church."** — No. Most Protestant communities reject the sacrificial dimension and most also reject the real presence in the Catholic sense. The Catholic Mass is a sui generis act.

## A summary in Saint Augustine's words

> *"O Sacrament of devotion! O sign of unity! O bond of charity!"*

The Mass is the central Christian act because it is the central Christian gift: **God himself given to his people, and his people given back to God in him.** Everything else in Christian life flows from it and back to it.

> «ይህ ቅዱስ ስጦታ — ሕይወታችንና አምልኮአችን — ምንጭ ነው።»
> — Sacrosanctum Concilium §10
