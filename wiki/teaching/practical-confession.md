# How to Make a Good Confession (የንስሐ ሥርዓት እንዴት እንደሚፈጸም)

**Type:** practical

**Compendium Q:** 295–312
**CCC:** 1422–1498
**Sources:** 5

## Synthesis

ንስሐ የክርስቶስ ስጦታ ነው፤ የተጠመቀ ሰው ካደረገው ኃጢአት ጋር ታርቆ የጸጋ ሕይወቱን እንዲቀጥል የተሰጠ ምሥጢር። ጥሩ ኑዛዜ የሚሠራው አምስት እርምጃዎች ሲሟሉ ነው፦ **ሕሊናን መመርመር፣ ንስሐ መግባት፣ ለመለወጥ መወሰን፣ ኃጢአትን መናዘዝ፣ የተሰጠውን ቀኖና መፈጸም**።

## The Five Steps (አምስቱ ደረጃዎች)

### 1. Examine your conscience (ሕሊናህን መርምር)

Sit quietly. Ask the Holy Spirit for light. Review your life since your last confession against the Ten Commandments and the Beatitudes.

### 2. Be sorry for your sins (ስለ ኃጢአትህ አዝን)

True sorrow has two forms:
- **Perfect contrition** — sorrow for sin because it offends God whom you love above all things.
- **Imperfect contrition** (attrition) — sorrow because of fear of hell or the ugliness of sin. This is sufficient for the sacrament.

### 3. Resolve to amend (ለመለወጥ ቁርጥ ውሳኔ ውሰድ)

Decide concretely: avoid this person, change this habit, leave this occasion of sin. A confession without resolve is incomplete.

### 4. Confess to a priest (ለካህን ተናዘዝ)

Approach the priest with humility. Begin:

> «በስመ አብ ወወልድ ወመንፈስ ቅዱስ አሐዱ አምላክ። አባቴ ባርከኝ፤ ኃጢአት ሠርቻለሁ።»
> "In the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Spirit. Bless me, Father; I have sinned."

State how long since your last confession. Then confess **all mortal sins by kind and number** — and any venial sins you wish. Don't excuse, don't blame. End with: *"For these and all the sins of my past life, I am sorry."*

### 5. Do the penance (ቀኖናህን ፈጽም)

The priest assigns a penance — usually prayers or an act of mercy. Do it as soon as possible. The penance does not "earn" forgiveness (Christ already won it on the cross) — it begins the work of repairing what sin damaged.

## Examination of Conscience by the Ten Commandments

Pray slowly. Ask: *Have I kept this commandment in thought, word, deed, and omission?*

### 1. I am the Lord your God; you shall not have other gods (እኔ እግዚአብሔር አምላክህ ነኝ)
- Have I prayed daily?
- Have I doubted God's love or his teaching deliberately?
- Have I trusted in horoscopes, charms, witchcraft (ጠንቋይ), or fortune-telling?
- Have I despaired of God's mercy or presumed upon it?
- Have I missed Mass on Sunday or a holy day through my own fault?

### 2. You shall not take the name of the Lord your God in vain (የእግዚአብሔር ስም በከንቱ አትጥራ)
- Have I cursed, blasphemed, or used God's name irreverently?
- Have I taken oaths lightly or broken vows?
- Have I spoken disrespectfully of holy things, the Church, or saints?

### 3. Remember to keep holy the Lord's day (ሰንበትን አክብር)
- Did I attend Mass attentively on Sundays and holy days?
- Did I work unnecessarily on Sunday?
- Did I keep Sunday as a day of rest, family, and prayer?

### 4. Honor your father and your mother (አባትህንና እናትህን አክብር)
- Have I honored, obeyed, and supported my parents?
- Have I shown respect to elders, teachers, civil and Church authorities?
- Have I cared for my children's faith and education?
- Have I been faithful in my duties of state in life?

### 5. You shall not kill (አትግደል)
- Have I harmed anyone in body or soul — anger, hatred, holding grudges?
- Have I been involved in abortion, supported it, or counseled it?
- Have I damaged my own health by drugs, drunkenness, gluttony, neglect?
- Have I forgiven those who hurt me?
- Have I been envious or wished evil on others?

### 6. You shall not commit adultery (አታመንዝር)
- Have I been faithful in my marriage in body and mind?
- Have I committed adultery, fornication, or any sexual sin?
- Have I used pornography or impure entertainment?
- Have I caused others to sin by dress, speech, or behavior?
- Have I used contraception against Church teaching?

### 7. You shall not steal (አትስረቅ)
- Have I taken what does not belong to me, no matter how small?
- Have I cheated in business, school, or taxes?
- Have I paid just wages and just prices?
- Have I made restitution for what I damaged or stole?
- Have I helped the poor according to my means?

### 8. You shall not bear false witness (በሐሰት አትመስክር)
- Have I lied — even to "spare feelings"?
- Have I gossiped, slandered, or detracted from another's reputation?
- Have I judged others rashly?
- Have I kept secrets entrusted to me?
- Have I been a hypocrite?

### 9. You shall not covet your neighbor's wife (የባልንጀራህን ሚስት አትመኝ)
- Have I entertained impure thoughts and desires?
- Have I guarded my eyes and imagination?

### 10. You shall not covet your neighbor's goods (የባልንጀራህን ንብረት አትመኝ)
- Have I been envious of what others have?
- Have I been greedy, stingy, or attached to wealth?
- Have I trusted God's providence in material things?

## After Absolution

When the priest gives absolution:

> «በአብ በወልድና በመንፈስ ቅዱስ ስም ኃጢአትህ ይማርልሃል። በሰላም ሂድ።»
> "I absolve you from your sins, in the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Spirit. Go in peace."

Make the sign of the cross. Leave quietly. Do your penance. Thank God — your soul has been washed clean.

## How often should I confess?

- **Required by Church law:** at least once a year if conscious of mortal sin.
- **Recommended:** monthly (devotional confession), even without mortal sin — for spiritual direction and growth.
- **Always before receiving Communion** if conscious of mortal sin.
