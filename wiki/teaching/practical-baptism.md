## Synthesis

ጥምቀት የክርስቲያን ሕይወት በር ነው። ሰው ከመጀመሪያ ኃጢአትና ከራሱ ኃጢአት የሚነጻበት፣ የእግዚአብሔር ልጅ የሚሆንበት፣ የክርስቶስ አባል፣ የመንፈስ ቅዱስ ቤተመቅደስና የቤተክርስቲያን አባል የሚሆንበት ምሥጢር ነው። ይህ ምሥጢር **አንዴ ብቻ የሚደረግ ዘላለማዊ ማኅተም** ነው — ሊደገም አይችልም።

## Who can be baptized?

- **Infants** of practicing Catholic parents — the Church baptizes infants because baptism is God's pure gift, not a reward for understanding.
- **Children** old enough to learn the basics of the faith.
- **Adults** who have been catechized, usually through the **RCIA** (Rite of Christian Initiation of Adults), receiving baptism, confirmation, and first Eucharist together at the Easter Vigil.

A previously baptized Christian (Orthodox, Protestant) who wishes to enter the Catholic Church is **not re-baptized** — baptism is one and unrepeatable. They make a profession of faith and are confirmed.

## What is needed for an infant baptism

### Documents
- Birth certificate of the child.
- Marriage certificate of parents (if married).
- Names of godparents and their proof of confirmation.

### Parents
- At least one parent must request baptism freely.
- There must be a **founded hope** that the child will be raised in the Catholic faith — at least one parent committed to practice. If there is no such hope, baptism is delayed (not refused) until catechetical conditions are present.
- Parents attend a pre-baptismal preparation class at the parish.

### Godparents (አባት ነፍስ / እናት ነፍስ)

Choose carefully. Godparents are spiritual mentors, not just friends or relatives at a celebration.

**Requirements** (Canon 874):
- At least 16 years old.
- A confirmed Catholic who has received First Communion and lives the faith.
- Not the parent of the child.
- Not under any canonical penalty.

**One Catholic godparent is required.** A second godparent of the opposite sex may be added. A baptized non-Catholic Christian (e.g., Ethiopian Orthodox) may serve as a **Christian witness** alongside the Catholic godparent — but not as the godparent.

## What is needed for an adult baptism

### The RCIA journey (የጎልማሶች ጥምቀት ጉዞ)

Traditionally a year-long process in four stages:

1. **Inquiry / Pre-catechumenate** — exploring the faith without commitment.
2. **Catechumenate** — formal instruction. The candidate becomes a *catechumen*. May last several months to several years depending on need.
3. **Purification and Enlightenment** — Lent of the year of baptism. Intense preparation, scrutinies, the handing over of the Creed and the Lord's Prayer.
4. **Mystagogy** — after baptism. Easter season of deepening into the mysteries received.

The actual baptism, confirmation, and first Eucharist are received together at the **Easter Vigil** (the night before Fasika).

## The Rite of Baptism — what happens

### Reception at the door
The priest welcomes the child (or adult) at the church entrance. Parents and godparents state the name and request baptism.

### Sign of the cross
The priest, parents, and godparents trace the cross on the forehead — claiming the child for Christ.

### Liturgy of the Word
Scripture readings on baptism. A short homily.

### Prayer of exorcism and anointing with oil of catechumens
Renouncing Satan, anointed for the spiritual battle.

### Blessing of water and renunciation of sin
Parents and godparents on behalf of the infant (or the adult personally) renounce Satan and profess faith in the Holy Trinity.

### The baptism itself
The priest pours water (or immerses) three times saying:

> «(ስም) የተባልክ፣ በአብ፣ በወልድና በመንፈስ ቅዱስ ስም አጠምቅሃለሁ።»
> "(Name), I baptize you in the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Spirit."

### Anointing with chrism (ሜሮን)
The priest anoints the crown of the head with sacred chrism — a sign that the baptized is now priest, prophet, and king in Christ.

### White garment
A white cloth is placed on the newly baptized — sign of having "put on Christ" (Galatians 3:27).

### Lighted candle
A candle, lit from the Easter (Paschal) candle, is given to the godparent — sign of the light of faith to be guarded until Christ returns.

### Blessing
Final blessing of the child, parents, and the assembly.

## After baptism — the lifelong journey

Baptism is the **beginning**. The baptized must be:

- **Catechized** — taught the faith year by year (Sunday school, religious education).
- **Brought to confession** when they reach the age of reason (around 7).
- **Brought to first Holy Communion** after appropriate preparation.
- **Brought to confirmation** to seal the baptismal grace with the gift of the Holy Spirit.
- **Living the faith** — Sunday Mass, daily prayer, sacramental life, life of charity.

## Practical questions

**Can a baby be baptized in danger of death?**
Yes. Anyone — even a non-Catholic — can baptize in emergency by pouring water on the head while saying the Trinitarian formula with the intention of doing what the Church does. Notify a priest as soon as possible afterward.

**What if the parents are not married in the Church?**
The child can still be baptized. The Church does not refuse the child for the parents' situation, but the parents are encouraged to regularize their marriage.

**What name can the child be given?**
A Christian name — a saint's name or a name with Christian meaning. Avoid names that are foreign to Christian sensibility.
