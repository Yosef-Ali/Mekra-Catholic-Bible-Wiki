## The Four

Christian tradition condenses **eschatology** — the study of last things — into four words a child can remember:

1. **ሞት** — Death
2. **ፍርድ** — Judgment
3. **ሰማይ** — Heaven
4. **ሲኦል** — Hell

To these four, the Catholic tradition adds a fifth state — **purgatory** (መንጻት) — for those who die in God's friendship but still need final purification before heaven. See [[apologetics/purgatory]].

The classic spiritual saying: *"Remember the four last things, and you will not sin forever."*

## 1. Death (ሞት)

### What death is

> «ሞት — የኃጢአት ደመወዝ ነው።»
> — ሮሜ 6:23

Death entered the world through sin (ዘፍ. 3:19, ሮሜ 5:12). It is not natural to the human person as God designed us — we were made for life. Yet in Christ death is **transformed**: no longer a final defeat, but the **gateway** to eternal life.

> «በክርስቶስ የሚሞቱ — ሕያዋን ናቸው።»
> — 1ኛ ቆሮ. 15:22

### What dies, what lives

The body dies; the soul does not. The soul is **immortal** by God's gift, created at conception, and goes immediately at death to its particular judgment (Q205). At the **end of time**, the body itself will rise again, transformed and reunited with the soul.

### How to prepare

The whole Christian life is preparation for death. The Latin tradition called this **memento mori** — *"remember you will die."* The point is not morbid; it is **clarifying**. A Christian who remembers death every day:

- Confesses regularly.
- Forgives quickly.
- Loves visibly.
- Stores treasure in heaven.
- Receives the Eucharist with attention.

> «ቀኑን አስቡ — ሰዓቱን አታውቁምና።»
> — ማቴ. 25:13

## 2. Judgment (ፍርድ)

The Church teaches **two judgments**:

### The Particular Judgment

At the moment of death, every soul appears before Christ alone, who renders judgment based on a life of faith and works. There are three possible outcomes:

- **Heaven** — for those in the state of grace, with no remaining attachments to sin.
- **Purgatory** — for those in the state of grace but still imperfectly purified.
- **Hell** — for those who die in mortal sin without repentance.

> «ለሰዎች ሞት የተወሰነ ነው — ከዚያም ፍርድ።»
> — ዕብ. 9:27

### The General (Last) Judgment

At the end of time, Christ returns in glory. All the dead rise; all are judged publicly. Bodies and souls are reunited. The full meaning of every deed is revealed — every secret hidden in the heart is laid bare (1ኛ ቆሮ. 4:5). The wicked depart into eternal punishment; the righteous into eternal life.

> «ሁላችን በክርስቶስ የፍርድ መንበር ፊት መገለጥ አለብን።»
> — 2ኛ ቆሮ. 5:10

The criteria of the Last Judgment Christ himself revealed (ማቴ. 25:31–46): the works of mercy. *"What you did to the least of these, you did to me."* See [[teaching/works-of-mercy]].

## 3. Heaven (ሰማይ)

### What heaven is

Not a place of rewards, but the **direct vision of God**. The Compendium (Q209) calls it *"perfect life with the Most Holy Trinity, communion of life and love with the Trinity, with the Virgin Mary, the angels, and all the blessed."*

Saint Paul describes it inadequately:

> «ዐይን ያላየ — ጆሮ ያልሰማ — በሰው ልብ ያልታሰበ — እግዚአብሔር ለሚወዱት ያዘጋጀላቸው ይህ ነው።»
> — 1ኛ ቆሮ. 2:9

### Who goes there

Those who die in God's grace and friendship, with no remaining attachment to sin. They are **fully purified**, ready to behold God face to face. They retain their personal identity — they are themselves, raised to glory — and they recognize one another.

### What they do

The saints in heaven:
- **Behold God** — the *beatific vision*. The fullness of joy.
- **Praise God** — the eternal liturgy of heaven (ራእይ 4–5).
- **Intercede for us** — see [[apologetics/praying-to-saints]].
- **Love perfectly** — every relationship rightly ordered, no resentment, no sin.

Heaven is **not** the absence of activity. It is activity at its full potential — but always rightly ordered toward God.

## 4. Hell (ሲኦል)

### What hell is

The Compendium (Q212) defines hell as **eternal separation from God**. It is not God who sends a soul to hell. It is the soul itself — refusing God definitively to the end — that chooses self over love.

> «ኦ! ብርጭቆዎች ካንሺኝ ይልቅ ቅኔዎች በኖር — እባርክ ኖሬያለሁ።»

The pain of hell is twofold:
- **Pain of loss** — never to see God, the deepest pain a creature made for God can suffer.
- **Pain of sense** — symbolized in Scripture as fire (ማቴ. 25:41) — a real suffering whose nature only the damned know.

### Who goes there

Those who die in **mortal sin** without repentance. Mortal sin requires **grave matter**, **full knowledge**, and **deliberate consent**. Without all three, the sin is venial.

### Does anyone go to hell?

The Church has never declared any specific person damned — even Judas. Catholic teaching holds out hope and prayer for every soul. **But the possibility of hell is real**, taught by Christ himself plainly and often (ማቴ. 25:41, ማር. 9:43–48, ሉቃ. 16:19–31). To deny hell is to deny Christ's own teaching.

The Christian must therefore live with **fear and trembling** (ፊል. 2:12) — not terror, but the holy reverence that takes salvation seriously.

### Hell as God's permission of human freedom

God does not want anyone in hell:

> «ሁሉም ለንስሐ እንዲደርሱ — ማንም እንዳይጠፋ — ይፈልጋል።»
> — 2ኛ ጴጥ. 3:9

Hell exists only because **God respects the freedom he gave us**. A love that cannot be refused is not love. A heaven imposed on someone who hates God would be hell to them. Hell is, in a tragic sense, mercy granting the soul its final wish: *"I will not serve."*

## The Five-Point Catholic Death Plan

Practical preparation, drawn from the Church's wisdom:

1. **Live in the state of grace.** Confess promptly when you fall.
2. **Receive the Last Rites if possible** — Confession, Eucharist (Viaticum), Anointing of the Sick. **Always call a priest** when a Catholic is gravely ill.
3. **Resign to God's will** — *"Into your hands I commend my spirit"* (ሉቃ. 23:46).
4. **Forgive everyone** — leave nothing unreconciled.
5. **Pray to Mary** — *"Holy Mary, Mother of God, pray for us sinners now and at the hour of our death."*

## What Catholics do for the dying and the dead

- **Holy Communion as Viaticum** — *"food for the journey"* — given to the dying.
- **Prayer at the moment of death** — never let a Catholic die alone if avoidable.
- **Funeral Mass** — offering Christ's sacrifice for the soul of the departed.
- **40-day prayer (አርባ)** — Ethiopian Catholic and Orthodox custom; the soul's transition.
- **Annual ተዝካር** — yearly memorial Mass on the anniversary of death.
- **Daily prayer for the dead** — at every Mass, the Church remembers the faithful departed.

The dead are not gone. They are **before us** on the path. They need our prayers; we need theirs. Death does not break the communion of saints — it deepens it.
