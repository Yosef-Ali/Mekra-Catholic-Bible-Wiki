## Overview
Zephaniah prophesies during the reign of King Josiah (7th century BC). His message is dominated by "the day of the Lord" — a day of wrath against Judah's idolatry but also a day of restoration for a humble remnant. The Latin hymn "Dies Irae" draws its imagery from Zephaniah. The book ends with one of Scripture's most tender images: God rejoicing over His people with singing (3:17).

## Key Themes
**The Day of Wrath.** "The great day of the Lord is near... a day of trumpet blast and battle cry" (1:14-16). Zephaniah's imagery entered Catholic liturgy through the Requiem Mass. The Church reads this not as terror alone but as the urgency of conversion — every Mass is preparation for the day of the Lord.

**The Humble Remnant.** "Seek the Lord, all you humble of the land" (2:3). Zephaniah promises that a faithful remnant — the 'anawim, the poor of the Lord — will survive judgment. Mary's Magnificat and the Beatitudes echo this theology of the humble as God's chosen.

## Key Passages
- Zephaniah 2:3 — Seek the Lord, all you humble
- Zephaniah 3:12 — I will leave a people humble and lowly
- Zephaniah 3:17 — He will rejoice over you with singing

## In the Compendium
Referenced in teaching on the final judgment and the communion of saints.
