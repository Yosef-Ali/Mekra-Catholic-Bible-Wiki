# Obadiah (ትንቢተ አብድዩ)
**Type:** bible | **Amharic:** ትንቢተ አብድዩ | **Chapters:** 1 | **Section:** OT
**Sources:** 2 | **Last updated:** 2026-05-06

## Overview
Obadiah, the shortest book in the Old Testament, is a single oracle against Edom for its betrayal of Judah during the Babylonian conquest. Edom "stood aloof" and "gloated" over Jerusalem's fall. The prophet declares that Edom will be destroyed and "the kingdom shall be the Lord's."

## Key Themes
**Divine Justice.** Edom's pride — dwelling in the clefts of the rock — cannot save it from God's judgment. The measure Edom used against its brother Jacob will be measured back. This principle of retributive justice grounds Catholic moral teaching on the duty of solidarity: to stand by while a neighbor suffers is itself a sin.

**Restoration of Zion.** The book ends with deliverance on Mount Zion and the kingdom belonging to the Lord — a messianic hope that the Church reads in light of Christ's universal reign.

## Key Passages
- Obadiah 1:3-4 — The pride of your heart has deceived you
- Obadiah 1:15 — As you have done, it shall be done to you
- Obadiah 1:21 — The kingdom shall be the Lord's
