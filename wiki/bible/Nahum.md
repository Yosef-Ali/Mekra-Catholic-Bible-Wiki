## Overview
Nahum is a triumphant oracle against Nineveh, capital of the Assyrian empire that had destroyed the northern kingdom of Israel. Where Jonah brought Nineveh repentance, Nahum brings its final sentence. The book celebrates God's justice against imperial oppression, affirming that no empire is beyond God's judgment.

## Key Themes
**God as Avenger.** "The Lord is slow to anger but great in power; the Lord will by no means clear the guilty" (1:3). Nahum presents God as the divine warrior who vindicates the oppressed. While the book's violent imagery challenges modern readers, Catholic tradition reads it as typological: the ultimate defeat of evil powers through Christ's cross.

**The Fall of the Oppressor.** Nineveh's fall is described with vivid poetry — chariots race, walls are breached, the lion's den is empty. "There is no healing for your wound" (3:19). This theme reinforces the Magnificat's promise that God "has put down the mighty from their thrones."
