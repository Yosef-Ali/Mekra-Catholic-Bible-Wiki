## Overview
Zechariah, a contemporary of Haggai, is the most messianic of the minor prophets. His eight night visions chart God's plan for restored Jerusalem. He foresees a king "humble and riding on a donkey" (9:9), a shepherd struck and the sheep scattered (13:7), and a fountain opened for cleansing from sin (13:1). The New Testament quotes Zechariah extensively in the Passion narratives and Revelation.

## Key Themes
**Messianic Visions.** Zechariah's "Branch" (3:8, 6:12) is a priest-king who builds the Temple. The humble king on a donkey (9:9) is quoted at Palm Sunday. The thirty pieces of silver (11:12-13) appears in Matthew's account of Judas. "They will look on him whom they have pierced" (12:10) is cited in John's Gospel at the crucifixion.

**Jerusalem Restored.** Zechariah envisions Jerusalem as the center of universal worship — "many peoples shall come to seek the Lord" (8:22). This eschatological vision shapes Catholic understanding of the Church as the New Jerusalem, the city set on a hill to which all nations are drawn.

## Key Passages
- Zechariah 9:9 — Your king comes, humble and riding on a donkey
- Zechariah 12:10 — They will look on him whom they have pierced
- Zechariah 13:1 — A fountain opened for sin and uncleanness
- Zechariah 14:9 — The Lord will be king over all the earth
