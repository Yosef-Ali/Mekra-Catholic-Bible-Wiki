## Overview
Colossians, written by Paul (or a close disciple in his name) from prison, addresses a church in Colossae threatened by a syncretistic "philosophy" that combined Jewish observances, angelic worship, and ascetic practices. The letter's response is a majestic Christology: Christ is "the image of the invisible God, the firstborn of all creation," in whom "all the fullness of deity dwells bodily" (2:9). He is the head of the body (the Church), the reconciliation of all things, and the mystery hidden for ages now revealed to the saints. The ethical instruction (chs. 3–4) flows from baptismal identity: "you have been raised with Christ; seek the things that are above."

## Key Themes
**The Cosmic Christ — Supremacy and Reconciliation.** Colossians 1:15–20 is a hymn to Christ as the agent, sustainer, and goal of all creation: "all things were created through him and for him." He is before all things, and in him all things hold together. He is the head of the body, the Church, and the firstborn from the dead. Through his blood of the cross, God reconciled "all things, whether on earth or in heaven." This cosmic Christology grounds the Catholic understanding that creation and redemption are united in Christ and that the Church participates in his cosmic reconciliation.

**Fullness in Christ versus Empty Deceit.** Paul counters the Colossian "philosophy" by insisting that in Christ "are hidden all the treasures of wisdom and knowledge" (2:3). He warns against being taken captive by human traditions, elemental spirits (στοιχεῖα), and regulations — "do not handle, do not taste, do not touch" (2:21). In Christ the whole fullness of deity dwells bodily, and believers have come to fullness in him (2:9–10). The old self has been put to death in baptism, and the new self is being renewed in knowledge after the image of its creator (3:9–10).

## Key Passages
- Colossians 1:15–16 — Christ is the image of the invisible God, firstborn of all creation
- Colossians 1:19–20 — Through him God reconciled all things, making peace by the blood of his cross
- Colossians 2:9 — In Christ the whole fullness of deity dwells bodily
- Colossians 3:1–2 — Seek the things that are above, where Christ is seated at the right hand of God
