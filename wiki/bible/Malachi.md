## Overview
Malachi, the last book of the Old Testament in the Christian canon, addresses a disillusioned post-exilic community. The Temple is rebuilt but the fervor has faded — priests offer blemished sacrifices and the people wonder if serving God is worth it. Malachi calls for purified worship and announces the coming of a messenger who will prepare the way. The book ends with the promise of Elijah's return, bridging the Testaments.

## Key Themes
**Pure Sacrifice.** Malachi condemns offering blind and lame animals while keeping the best for oneself (1:6-14). In response, God promises a "pure offering" from the rising of the sun to its setting (1:11). The Council of Trent and the Catholic liturgy read this as a prophecy of the Mass — the one pure sacrifice offered worldwide.

**The Messenger and the Day.** "Behold, I send my messenger to prepare the way before me" (3:1) is applied to John the Baptist in all four Gospels. The "sun of righteousness" rising with healing in its wings (4:2) is a beloved Advent image. Elijah's promised return (4:5) finds fulfillment in John the Baptist.
