# Fourth Commandment Page Has Seventh Commandment Q&A Content

**Type:** qa
**Date:** 2026-05-06
**Question:** Why does `wiki/teaching/fourth-commandment.md` contain Q&A 503–509 about the seventh commandment (property/stealing)?
**Last updated:** 2026-05-06
**Related:** [[teaching/fourth-commandment]], [[teaching/seventh-and-tenth-commandments]]

## Answer

The teaching page titled **"Fourth Commandment (አራተኛይቱ ትእዛዝ)"** contains Compendium Q&A items 503–509, which all cover the **Seventh Commandment** — the prohibition on stealing, private property, economic justice, treatment of animals, and the Church's social teaching. The title and the content do not match.

### What the page actually covers

Every Q&A on the page is about the Seventh Commandment:

| Q# | Topic | Key Content |
|----|-------|-------------|
| Q503 | What the seventh commandment requires | Distribution of wealth, private property, respect for creation |
| Q504 | Conditions for private property | Justly acquired, benefits all |
| Q505 | Purpose of private property | Freedom, dignity, helping the poor |
| Q506 | What the seventh commandment demands | Justice, charity, honoring contracts, restitution |
| Q507 | Treatment of animals | Creatures of God, avoid cruelty |
| Q508 | What the seventh commandment forbids | Theft, fraud, usury, price manipulation |
| Q509 | Church social teaching | Human dignity, social dimension |

### What the Fourth Commandment should cover

The Fourth Commandment is "Honor your father and mother" (አባትህንና እናትህን አክብር፤ ኦሪት ዘፀአት 20:12). Its Compendium Q&A range should cover family, parental authority, duties of children, and civil authority (CCC 2196–2257), not property and stealing.

### How this happened

During the initial Compendium ingest (2026-04-09), Q&A items 503–509 were assigned to `fourth-commandment.md` instead of a Seventh Commandment page. A separate page `wiki/teaching/seventh-and-tenth-commandments.md` exists but covers a different Q range. The mapping error is likely due to Q numbering confusion during the bulk ingest.

The page itself flags the mismatch in its **Open questions** section and carries a `[Note]` under Scripture acknowledging the content discrepancy.
