## The Question

Why do Catholics confess sins to a priest? Can't we confess directly to God? 1 John 1:9 says if we confess, God is faithful to forgive. Psalm 32:5 shows David confessing directly. Why insert a human mediator between the sinner and God?

## The Catholic Response

Catholics do confess directly to God — that is the foundation of every sacramental confession. But Christ established a specific, visible means of forgiveness for post-baptismal sins. After the Resurrection, Jesus breathed on the apostles and said: "Receive the Holy Spirit. If you forgive the sins of any, they are forgiven; if you retain the sins of any, they are retained" (Jn 20:22-23). This is the only place where Christ explicitly delegates His divine authority to forgive sins.

The apostles were given the power to forgive *or retain*. To make that judgment, they must hear sins confessed — they cannot read hearts. This is why the sacrament requires oral confession: the priest, acting *in persona Christi*, hears the confession, offers counsel, assigns penance, and pronounces absolution. This is not a human invention but Christ's own provision. The priest is not a barrier but a channel of mercy — a visible assurance that sins are truly forgiven by Christ's authority working through His Church.

## Scriptural Foundation

> ይህንንም ብሎ እፍ አላቸውና "መንፈስ ቅዱስን ተቀበሉ፤ ኃጢአታቸውን ይቅር ላላችኋቸው ሁሉ ይቀርላቸዋል፤ የያዛችሁባቸው ተይዘውባቸዋል፤" አላቸው። (ዮሐንስ 20፡22-23)

Jesus gave the apostles authority to forgive and retain sins. This requires hearing specific sins to exercise that judgment. The Church continues this apostolic ministry through ordained priests.

> All this is from God, who through Christ reconciled us to himself and gave us the ministry of reconciliation. (2 Corinthians 5:18)

Paul teaches that God gave the apostles a *ministry of reconciliation*. They serve as ambassadors for Christ, through whom God continues reconciling the world to Himself. *(N.b.: DB lookup for 2 Cor 5:18 Amharic returned a mismatched verse; the English is used here as the canonical text.)*
