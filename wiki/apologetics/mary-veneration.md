## The Question

Why do Catholics give Mary such a prominent role — calling her "Mother of God," "Queen of Heaven," and "Mediatrix"? Some argue this elevates Mary to divine status and amounts to idolatry. Doesn't Mary herself direct attention away from herself and toward her Son?

## The Catholic Response

Catholic veneration of Mary is deeply biblical and carefully distinguished from worship. The Church uses three Greek terms: *latria* (worship, God alone), *dulia* (honor of saints), and *hyperdulia* (highest honor, Mary alone). Mary is not worshiped — she is honored for what God accomplished through her. Her Magnificat proclaims: "My soul magnifies the Lord" (Lk 1:46). Everything Mary does points to Christ.

Mary is *Theotokos* (God-bearer) because she gave birth to Jesus Christ — one divine Person with two natures. The Council of Ephesus (431 AD) defended this title precisely to protect the truth that Jesus is truly God and truly man. To deny Mary as Mother of God divides Christ's person. All Marian doctrines — Immaculate Conception, perpetual virginity, Assumption — safeguard truths about Christ and His Church. Mary is the perfect disciple, the model of the Church, and the first to be fully redeemed. When Jesus gave Mary to the beloved disciple at the cross (Jn 19:26-27), He gave her as mother to all faithful Christians.

## Scriptural Foundation

> እኔን ዝቅተኛይቱን አገልጋይ ተመልክቷልና፤ እነሆም፥ ከዛሬ ጀምሮ ትውልድ ሁሉ ብፅዕት ይሉኛል። (ሉቃስ 1፡48)

Mary prophesied under the Holy Spirit that "all generations will call me blessed." To refuse to honor her is to resist biblical prophecy. Elizabeth, also Spirit-filled, cried: "Blessed are you among women, and blessed is the fruit of your womb!" (Lk 1:42).

> ኢየሱስም እናቱን የሚወደውንም ደቀ መዝሙር በአጠገቡ ቆሞ ባየ ጊዜ እናቱን "አንቺ ሴት፥ እነሆ ልጅሽ" አላት። ከዚያም ደቀ መዝሙሩን "እነሆ እናትህ" አለው። (ዮሐንስ 19፡26-27)

On the cross, Jesus entrusted Mary to the beloved disciple — representing all faithful Christians. Mary is our spiritual mother by Christ's own gift.
