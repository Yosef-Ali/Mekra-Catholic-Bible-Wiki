# Sola Scriptura? — Scripture Alone?

**Type:** apologetics
**Question:** Is the Bible alone the only authority for Christian faith?
**Compendium Q:** 11–17
**CCC:** 80–87
**Sources:** 5
**Last updated:** 2026-05-09
**Related:** [[teaching/faith-and-revelation]], [[apologetics/deuterocanon]], [[apologetics/papacy-pope-primacy]], [[apologetics/apostolic-succession]]

## The challenge

> *"The Bible is the only authority. Everything must be proved from Scripture alone (Sola Scriptura). Catholic traditions are man-made additions."*

This is the foundational Protestant principle, articulated by Luther in the 16th century. Examined carefully, it is **self-refuting**, **historically false**, and **rejected by Scripture itself**.

## Problem 1 — Sola Scriptura is not in the Bible

The first and most decisive problem: **the Bible nowhere teaches Sola Scriptura**.

The most-cited verse in defense of it is:

> «መጽሐፍ ሁሉ በእግዚአብሔር መንፈስ ተጻፈ — ለማስተማር — ለመውቀስ — ለማቅናት — በጽድቅ ለመገሠጽ ይጠቅማል። ለበጎ ሥራ ሁሉ የተዘጋጀ ይሆን ዘንድ።»
> — 2ኛ ጢሞቴዎስ 3:16–17

But this verse:
- **Says Scripture is "useful" (ὠφέλιμος, profitable) — not "the only source."**
- **Refers primarily to the Old Testament**, since the New Testament wasn't yet collected when Paul wrote it.
- **Says the man of God is "complete"** — not that Scripture by itself is the complete deposit.
- **Logically does not exclude tradition.** "X is profitable for Y" does not mean "X is the only thing profitable for Y."

If Scripture is the *only* authority, Scripture must teach this. It does not. The principle Sola Scriptura **violates itself**.

## Problem 2 — Scripture explicitly affirms tradition

> «ስለዚህ ወንድሞች ሆይ — ጽኑ ሁኑ።
> በቃላችንም ቢሆን — በመልእክታችንም ቢሆን —
> የተማራችሁትን **ትውፊት ጠብቁ።**»
> — 2ኛ ተሰሎንቄ 2:15

Paul tells the Thessalonians to hold fast to traditions handed down **either by his preached word or by his letter** — i.e., oral tradition is on the same plane as written Scripture.

> «የመረከባችሁን ትውፊት ሁሉ የሚተው ወንድም ሁሉ ራቁ።»
> — 2ኛ ተሰሎንቄ 3:6

> «የመረከባችሁን ምሥራች ለሌላ የሚሰጥ ቢኖር — እርሱ የተረገመ ይሁን — ከሰማይ መልአክ ቢሆን እንኳን።»
> — ገላትያ 1:8–9

The criterion is the **received apostolic deposit** — not anyone's personal Bible interpretation.

## Problem 3 — The Bible itself was given by tradition

The list of which books are inspired Scripture is **not in any book of the Bible**. Nowhere does the Bible say "these 27 books are the New Testament." That list — the **canon** — was determined by the Catholic Church through the Councils of Rome, Hippo, and Carthage (382–419 AD).

So a Protestant who holds Sola Scriptura must accept a list of books **on the authority of the Catholic Church and its tradition** — and then turn around and reject the authority that gave them the list.

This is a contradiction at the foundation.

## Problem 4 — The Church existed before the Bible

For roughly **20 years** after Christ's resurrection, there was no New Testament. Christians lived, preached, baptized, celebrated the Eucharist, and died as martyrs **without a single New Testament book**. They had:

- The **oral preaching of the apostles**.
- The **Old Testament** (in the Septuagint).
- **Liturgical and sacramental practice** received from the apostles.

Even after the New Testament was written, **the canon was not finalized for 350+ years**. For three and a half centuries the Christian Church functioned without a finalized Bible — and yet was guarded from heresy by what?

By **the apostolic Tradition**, taught and protected by the apostolic Church.

## Problem 5 — The pillar of truth

> «የእውነት ምሰሶና መደገፊያ — የእግዚአብሔር ቤት — ቤተክርስቲያን — ናት።»
> — 1ኛ ጢሞቴዎስ 3:15

Paul names the **Church**, not the Bible, as the pillar and foundation of truth. The Bible is in the Church, the Church is not in the Bible.

## Problem 6 — Sola Scriptura produces 30,000 denominations

If Scripture interpreted by each individual is sufficient, why are there over 30,000 Protestant denominations — each claiming to follow Scripture alone — and disagreeing on baptism, the Eucharist, salvation, ordination, marriage, ethics, and church governance?

Christ prayed: *"That they may all be one"* (ዮሐ. 17:21). Sola Scriptura has produced division on a scale unknown to apostolic Christianity.

## The Catholic position

Catholics hold to **three** mutually supporting authorities, each working with the other two:

1. **Sacred Scripture** — the inspired written Word of God.
2. **Sacred Tradition** — the oral teaching of the apostles, lived in the Church's life, liturgy, and Fathers.
3. **The Magisterium** — the teaching authority of the bishops in union with the Pope, given by Christ to interpret Scripture and Tradition authentically.

> «ይህም መልእክት — ቅዱስ ትውፊትና ቅዱስ መጽሐፍ — እርስ በርሳቸው ተቀባብለው ይታያሉ — ኅብረትም ይኖራቸዋል። ምክንያቱም ሁለቱም — ከእግዚአብሔር ሲወጡ — ወደ እግዚአብሔር ይደርሳሉ።»
> — *Dei Verbum* §9 (Vatican II)

These three are not three competing authorities; they are one tripod of revelation. Removing one collapses the whole.

## A practical illustration

Ask a Protestant who holds Sola Scriptura:

- **Where does the Bible list the 27 books of the New Testament?** *(Nowhere — the Church listed them.)*
- **Where does the Bible teach Sola Scriptura?** *(Nowhere.)*
- **Why don't you keep Saturday Sabbath, since the Bible doesn't change it?** *(Because the Church changed the day to Sunday.)*
- **Why is your canon different from Luther's, who rejected James?** *(Because Tradition keeps you from Luther's idiosyncrasies.)*

In each case, the Protestant is implicitly relying on **Catholic Tradition** to know what the Bible is and how to read it.

## The Ethiopian context

Ethiopian Christians — Orthodox and Catholic alike — have always held that the **Ge'ez Scriptures**, the **Liturgy of the Cherubim**, the **synaxarium** (lives of saints), and the **canons of the Apostles and Fathers** are received together as a single living deposit. The Sola Scriptura principle was never part of Ethiopian Christianity. It is a 16th-century European innovation.
