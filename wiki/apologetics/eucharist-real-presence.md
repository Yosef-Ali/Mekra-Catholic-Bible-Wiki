# Is the Eucharist Really the Body and Blood of Christ?
**Type:** apologetics
**Amharic:** ቁርባን በእውነት የክርስቶስ ሥጋና ደም ነውን?
**Compendium Q:** 271–294 (especially 282–284)
**Sources:** 4
**Last updated:** 2026-05-06
**Related:** [[teaching/eucharist]], [[teaching/passion-death-resurrection]]

## The Question

How can Catholics claim the Eucharist is literally Christ's body and blood? It still looks, tastes, and feels like bread and wine. Isn't Jesus speaking symbolically — just as He said "I am the door" (Jn 10:9) and "I am the vine" (Jn 15:5)? Why believe something that contradicts our senses?

## The Catholic Response

Catholics do not deny that the appearances of bread and wine remain. The Church teaches *transubstantiation*: by Christ's words at consecration, the entire substance is changed into His Body and Blood, while accidents (appearance, taste, texture) remain. This is not a contradiction of reason but a mystery that transcends it — exactly what we should expect from God-made-man.

The decisive text is John 6. Jesus repeatedly declares: "My flesh is true food, my blood is true drink." When many disciples murmur and leave, He does not clarify or soften His words — He lets them walk away. If He meant symbolism, this was the moment to explain; instead He doubles down. At the Last Supper, He says "this IS my body" (Matt 26:26), not "this represents." Paul warns that unworthy eating makes one "guilty of the body and blood of the Lord" (1 Cor 11:27) — you cannot be guilty of a symbol. The Eucharist is Calvary made sacramentally present: not re-sacrifice but re-presentation of Christ's one eternal sacrifice.

## Scriptural Foundation

> ኢየሱስም እንዲህ አላቸው «እውነት እውነት እላችኋለሁ፤ የሰው ልጅን ሥጋ ካልበላችሁ ደሙንም ካልጠጣችሁ ... ሥጋዬን የሚበላ ደሜንም የሚጠጣ የዘላለም ሕይወት አለው፤ እኔም በመጨረሻው ቀን አስነሣዋለሁ። ሥጋዬ እውነተኛ መብል ደሜም እውነተኛ መጠጥ ነውና። ሥጋዬን የሚበላ ደሜንም የሚጠጣ በእኔ ይኖራል፤ እኔም በእርሱ እኖራለሁ።» (ዮሐንስ 6፡53-56)

Jesus intensifies rather than explains away the realism. The Greek verb used (*trogo*) means "gnaw, chew" — not a polite term for eating. Disciples abandoned Him over this; He let them go.

> ስለዚህ ያለአግባብ ይህን ኅብስት የበላ ወይም የጌታን ጽዋ የጠጣ ሁሉ የጌታ ሥጋና ደም ዕዳ አለበት። ... ሳይገባው የሚበላና የሚጠጣ የጌታን ሥጋ ስለማይለይ ለራሱ ፍርድ ይበላል፤ ይጠጣል። (1ኛ ቆሮንቶስ 11፡27, 29)

Paul says the unworthy communicant is *guilty of the body and blood of the Lord*. Juridical guilt over a mere symbol is incoherent. Failure to "discern the body" brings judgment — some in Corinth had died (v. 30).
