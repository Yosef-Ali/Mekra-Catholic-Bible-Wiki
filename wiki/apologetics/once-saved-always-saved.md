# "Once Saved, Always Saved"?

**Type:** apologetics
**Question:** Once a person is saved, can they ever lose their salvation?
**Compendium Q:** 422–425, 391–395
**CCC:** 161–162, 1854–1864
**Sources:** 4
**Last updated:** 2026-05-09
**Related:** [[teaching/sin]], [[teaching/moral-law-and-grace]], [[apologetics/sola-fide]], [[concepts/ጽድቅ]]

## The challenge

> *"If I have once truly believed in Jesus, I am saved forever. Nothing I do can separate me from God's love. To say I can lose salvation is to deny the assurance of the Gospel."*

This doctrine — popularly called **"Once Saved, Always Saved"** (OSAS) or **eternal security** — is widely taught in Baptist, Pentecostal, and Evangelical circles. It rests on a few proof texts read in isolation, and it is **directly contradicted** by Christ, the apostles, and 2,000 years of Christian tradition.

## What Scripture actually says

### 1. Christ's clear warnings

> «የጸናው እስከ መጨረሻ — እርሱ ይድናል።»
> — ማቴ. 24:13

Christ does not say *"once saved, always saved"*. He says **"the one who endures to the end"** is the one saved. This implies the possibility of *not* enduring.

> «የቆረቆር ብእሲ — በወይኑ የሚተከል ቅርንጫፍ ሁሉ —
> ፍሬ የማያፈራውን — ይቆርጣል —
> ፍሬ የሚያፈራውንም — ይቆረጥማል — እንዲበዛ።»
> — ዮሐ. 15:2

> «በእኔ የማይኖር — እንደ ቅርንጫፍ ይጣላል — ይደርቃል።
> ይሰበስቡታል — በእሳትም ይጥሉታል — ይቃጠላልም።»
> — ዮሐ. 15:6

A branch was **once attached** to the vine. Christ describes branches that **were in him** and have been **cut off** and burned. If a person was never saved, they were never a branch in the vine in the first place. **The text requires that some genuine disciples can be lost.**

### 2. Paul's warnings — even of himself

> «ራሴን — ጠንቅቄ — እመታለሁ — እገዛለሁ።
> ለሌሎች ስሰብክ — እኔ ራሴ — በፊቴ ባላወቀ — እንዳልቀር።»
> — 1ኛ ቆሮ. 9:27

Paul — the apostle to the Gentiles — fears **his own** disqualification. If Paul could fall away, who cannot?

> «በእምነት የቆማችሁ — ላለመውደቃችሁ ተጠንቀቁ።»
> — 1ኛ ቆሮ. 10:12

The verse is incoherent under OSAS — why warn the saved against falling if falling is impossible?

> «ከእምነት የራቁት — የመንፈስን — ቀንበሮች የሚሰሙ —
> በውሸተኛ መናፍስቶች — በአጋንንትም ትምህርት — የተታለሉ ናቸው።»
> — 1ኛ ጢሞ. 4:1

People depart **from the faith** — they had it, and they left it.

### 3. Hebrews — the strongest passage

> «ብርሃኑን ለቀመሱ — የሰማይ ስጦታን ላልተካፈሉ —
> የመንፈስ ቅዱስም ኅብረት ላገኙ —
> የእግዚአብሔርን ቃል — የሚመጣውን ዓለም ኃይል — ቀምሰው —
> በኋላ የደጉት —
> ለንስሐ ዳግም መታደስ — የማይቻል ነው።»
> — ዕብራውያን 6:4–6

The Hebrews passage describes people who:
- Have been **enlightened**.
- Have **tasted the heavenly gift**.
- Have become **partakers of the Holy Spirit**.
- Have **tasted the goodness of the word of God**.

These are **saved Christians**. And the text says they **fell away**.

> «የእውነት እውቀት ከደረስን በኋላ — ሆን ብለን ኃጢአት ብናደርግ —
> ስለ ኃጢአት መሥዋዕት ሌላ የለም —
> የሚያስፈራ የፍርድ መጠባበቅ ብቻ ነው።»
> — ዕብራውያን 10:26–27

> «ጽድቅን ካወቁ በኋላ — ከተሰጣቸው ቅዱስ ትእዛዝ — የሚመለሱ ሰዎች —
> ቅዱስ ትእዛዝን ካለማወቅ ይሻላቸው ነበር።»
> — 2ኛ ጴጥ. 2:21

### 4. The unforgivable sin

> «በመንፈስ ቅዱስ ላይ የሚሳደብ ቢሆን —
> በዚህም ዓለም ቢሆን — በሚመጣው ዓለምም — ይቅር አይባልም።»
> — ማቴ. 12:32

Christ teaches that **certain sins** can be unforgivable. If salvation cannot be lost, this verse makes no sense.

### 5. The branches of Romans 11

> «የተወሰኑ ቅርንጫፎች ቢቆረጡ —
> አንተ — የዱር ወይራ — በመካከላቸው ተተከልክ።
> ስለዚህ — እግዚአብሔር ለተፈጥሮ ቅርንጫፎች ካልራራ —
> ለአንተም አይራራም።
> በመስጠት ቆመህ — እምነትህን — ካልጠበቅህ — እንደነዚህ ትቆረጥ።»
> — ሮሜ 11:21–22

Paul tells **Christians** they can be **cut off** if they do not continue in faith and obedience.

## The logical problem

OSAS makes the **"true believer"** impossible to identify in advance. When a self-professed believer falls away into apostasy, denial, or grave sin, the OSAS Christian must say: *"They were never really saved in the first place"* — which means **no one can ever know they are saved**, because they may yet fall away and be revealed as never having been saved.

This destroys the very assurance OSAS claims to provide.

The Catholic answer is more honest: **you can know you are in a state of grace right now, and you must continue in faith to remain so.**

## What about "no one can pluck them out of my hand" (John 10:28)?

> «ለዘላለም አይጠፉም —
> ማንም — ከእጄ — አይነጥቃቸውም።»
> — ዮሐ. 10:28

The verse says no one can take a sheep from Christ's hand. **It does not say a sheep cannot leave on its own.** Christ does not violate human freedom; he loves us, but he does not coerce us. The very next chapter has him weeping over Jerusalem: *"How often would I have gathered your children, but you would not!"* (Mt. 23:37). God offered. Jerusalem refused.

The Catholic position: **God does not lose any sheep — but a sheep can desert the shepherd.**

## What about "I am persuaded that nothing can separate us from the love of Christ" (Romans 8:38–39)?

> «ሞትም ቢሆን — ሕይወትም — መላእክትም —
> ግዛቶችም — የአሁኑም — የሚመጣውም — ኃይሎችም —
> ጥልቀትም — ከፍታም — ሌላ ፍጡርም —
> በክርስቶስ ኢየሱስ — በጌታችን — ካለው ከእግዚአብሔር ፍቅር —
> ሊለየን አይችልም።»
> — ሮሜ 8:38–39

Paul lists what cannot separate us from God's love: external forces — death, life, angels, principalities, height, depth, things present, things to come.

**Notice what is not on the list: ourselves.** Paul does not say *"nor my own free will"* or *"nor my own grave sin."* The one thing that *can* separate us from God's love is **our own free decision to abandon him through grave sin**.

## The Catholic position

1. **Salvation is by grace alone, through faith** that works in love.
2. **The justified can lose their salvation through mortal sin** — the deliberate, knowing rejection of God in a grave matter.
3. **Confession restores the lost grace** — this is why the sacrament of penance is necessary.
4. **Final salvation requires perseverance to the end** — *"He who endures to the end will be saved"* (ማቴ. 24:13).
5. **A Christian can have moral certainty** of being in a state of grace right now (frequent confession, no mortal sin on conscience), but not the **absolute certainty** of final salvation that would make perseverance unnecessary.

This is the universal teaching of:
- The early Church Fathers — without exception.
- The Catholic Church.
- The Eastern Orthodox Churches.
- The Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahedo Church.
- The Anglican and Methodist traditions.
- The Lutheran tradition (despite Luther's polemics on related points).

The OSAS doctrine is a **Calvinist innovation** of the 16th–17th century, rejected by the rest of Christianity.

## A pastoral note

OSAS sounds comforting but it is dangerous:

- It can lead to **moral laxity** — *"My past sins don't matter; my future sins won't either"*.
- It can lead to **despair** when a Christian commits grave sin and is told *"You were never really saved."*
- It cuts the nerve of **lifelong vigilance** — the watchfulness Christ commands repeatedly.

The Catholic teaching is harder, but truer: **God's love is unconditional; our cooperation must be daily.**

> «ራሳቸውን — ራሳቸውን አስተውሉ —
> በእምነት ወይ አይደላችሁ —
> በእምነት ብትሆኑ — ራሳችሁን ፈትሹ።»
> — 2ኛ ቆሮ. 13:5

Paul tells the Corinthians to **examine** themselves — implying that some of them may not, in fact, be in the faith they think they are in.

## How a Catholic walks daily

- **Confession** — at least monthly. The sacrament restores grace lost to mortal sin.
- **Communion in the state of grace** — never in mortal sin.
- **Daily prayer** — strength against temptation.
- **Watchfulness** — the seven capital sins are always at the door.
- **Spiritual direction** — a wise priest or guide.
- **Trust in God's mercy** — without presumption.

This is the **narrow road** Christ describes (ማቴ. 7:14). Not gloomy, not anxious, but *vigilant* — confident in grace, alert to one's own weakness.
