# Why Call Priests "Father"?

**Type:** apologetics
**Question:** Doesn't Matthew 23:9 forbid calling anyone "father"?
**CCC:** 871–873, 1547
**Sources:** 4
**Last updated:** 2026-05-09
**Related:** [[teaching/holy-orders]], [[concepts/ክህነት]], [[apologetics/apostolic-succession]]

## The challenge

> *"Jesus said, 'Call no man your father on earth, for you have one Father, who is in heaven' (Matthew 23:9). Catholics violate this by calling priests 'Father.'"*

A common objection. It dissolves the moment you read the Bible.

## What Christ actually says

> «በምድር ላይ ማንንም ሰው አባት አትበሉ —
> በሰማይ ያለ — አንድ ብቻ አባታችሁ ነውና።
> "ረቢ" ተብሎም አትጠሩ —
> አንድ ብቻ መምህራችሁ — ክርስቶስ — ነውና።
> "መምህር"ም አትባሉ —
> አንድ ብቻ መምህራችሁ — ክርስቶስ — ነውና።»
> — ማቴዎስ 23:8–10

If this is taken **literally and absolutely**, then:

- We cannot call our biological father "father." (You'd have to call him by his name.)
- We cannot call any teacher "teacher" or "rabbi."
- We cannot use "Mister," "Doctor," "Professor," "Reverend" — all of which mean "teacher" or "master."

**No Protestant — no Christian of any kind — actually lives this way.**

Why? Because Christ is using **rhetorical hyperbole** to make a point about pride and exclusive loyalty. The same Bible that records this saying also:

- Calls **Abraham** "our father" — Romans 4:16, James 2:21
- Calls Paul **"father"** of his converts — *"I became your father in Christ Jesus through the gospel"* (1 ቆሮ. 4:15)
- Calls believers spiritual **"sons"** of their teachers — *"my son Timothy"* (2 ጢሞ. 1:2)
- Refers to **Eliab** as Israel's "father" — Jud. 17:10
- Has Stephen call the Sanhedrin members **"fathers"** — Acts 7:2

If "Father" was strictly forbidden, **the New Testament writers themselves are violating Christ's command**.

## What Christ actually meant

Christ is in Matthew 23 condemning the **Pharisees' love of titles** for self-exaltation. They liked being called "Father" and "Rabbi" because it puffed up their egos and obscured the truth that all spiritual authority comes from God.

The point is not the words "Father" and "Teacher" themselves; it is the **abuse** of those titles to claim authority that does not belong to oneself. Christ explains this clearly in the same passage:

> «ከእናንተ ታላቅ የሆነ — የእናንተ አገልጋይ ይሆናል።
> ራሱን ከፍ የሚያደርግ — ይዋረዳል —
> ራሱን ዝቅ የሚያደርግ — ከፍ ይላል።»
> — ማቴዎስ 23:11–12

The condemnation is of **prideful self-importance**, not of the natural words by which families and spiritual families recognize their bonds.

## Why "Father" is the right title for a priest

### 1. The priest is a spiritual father

Paul uses **fatherhood language** for his ministry deliberately:

> «በክርስቶስ አሥር ሺ መምህራን ቢኖሩ — ብዙ አባት የላችሁም።
> በወንጌል እኔ በክርስቶስ ኢየሱስ — **አባታችሁ — ሆኜአለሁ**።»
> — 1ኛ ቆሮንቶስ 4:14–15

Paul is the spiritual father of the Corinthians because he begot them in the Gospel.

The priest:
- Begets new Christians at baptism (regenerative fatherhood).
- Forgives sins in the confessional (the prodigal son's father).
- Feeds his children with the Eucharist.
- Provides the family meal of the Mass.
- Teaches, corrects, and protects the flock.

This is a **paternal role**, not just a friendly one. The title fits the function.

### 2. The Old Testament called priests "father"

When Micah hires a Levite to serve as priest in his household:

> «ለእኔ — አባትና — ካህን — ሁነኝ።»
> — መሣፍንት 17:10

The connection between **priesthood** and **fatherhood** runs through the whole Bible. It is not a Catholic invention.

### 3. The early Church used this language

- **St. Paul** — "father" of his converts.
- **St. John** — calls his readers *"my little children"* repeatedly (1 John 2:1, 2:12, etc.).
- **Stephen** addresses the Sanhedrin: *"Brothers and **fathers**, hear me"* (Acts 7:2).
- **The Desert Fathers** (3rd–5th c.) — bore the title *Abba* ("father") universally.
- **The Patriarchate of Alexandria** (the see Athanasius held) — the head was called *"Papa"* / *"Pope"* / *"Father"* from the 3rd century. The Ethiopian Orthodox patriarch is still called **አቡነ** ("our father") today.

The use of "Father" for spiritual leaders is **older than the New Testament canon was finalized**.

## A specific Ethiopian observation

Ethiopian Christians — Orthodox and Catholic — universally address priests as **አባ** ("Father"). The title is not an importation from European Catholicism; it is the ancient Christian East's natural language. Calling a priest "Brother" or by his first name without title would be jarring in Ethiopian liturgical and pastoral context.

## What is forbidden

What Christ truly forbids is:

- **Spiritual pride** — claiming authority that comes only from God.
- **Self-exaltation** — using titles to set oneself above the brethren.
- **Replacing God** — making any human being the ultimate source of one's spiritual life.

A priest who is called "Father" must remember he is so only as a **steward of the one Father** in heaven — pointing always beyond himself to God.

## A test question for the objector

Ask: *"Do you call your biological father 'father'?"*

- If **yes** — they are inconsistent in their reading of Matthew 23:9.
- If **no** — they would be the only person in human history to obey their reading.

Either way, the literal-absolute reading collapses, and the Catholic position is left standing: **Christ condemned proud titles, not paternal language for spiritual leaders.**
