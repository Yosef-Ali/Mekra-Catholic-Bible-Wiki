## The Question

Many Christians ask: Why do Catholics pray to Mary and the saints instead of praying directly to God? Doesn't 1 Timothy 2:5 say there is "one mediator between God and men, the man Christ Jesus"? Does praying to saints violate the worship due to God alone?

## The Catholic Response

Catholics do not pray to saints in the same way they pray to God. "Prayer" (from Latin *precari*, "to ask") means to entreat. When we ask fellow Christians on earth to pray for us, no one accuses us of worshiping them instead of God. The saints in heaven are alive in Christ — more alive than we are. Scripture shows they present our prayers to God (Rev 5:8).

The distinction is between *latria* (worship due to God alone) and *dulia* (honor given to saints). We ask saints to intercede just as we ask friends to pray for us — only the saints, perfected in charity, are more effective intercessors (James 5:16). This is the "communion of saints" of the Apostles' Creed: the unity of the Church on earth, in purgatory, and in heaven. Death does not sever Christ's Body (Rom 8:38-39).

## Scriptural Foundation

> መጽሐፉንም በወሰደ ጊዜ አራቱ ሕያዋን ፍጡራንና ሃያ አራቱ ሽማግሌዎች በበጉ ፊት ወደቁ፤ እያንዳንዳቸውም በገናና የቅዱሳን ጸሎት የሆነ ዕጣን የሞላበትን የወርቅ ጽዋን ይዘው ነበር። (ራእይ 5፡8)

The elders in heaven hold "golden bowls full of incense, which are the prayers of the saints" — presenting the prayers of Christians on earth to God.

> እንግዲህ እንደዚህ ዓይነት ምስክሮች እንደ ደመና በዙሪያችን ካሉን፥ እኛ ደግሞ ማንኛውንም ሸክምና የተጠላለፈብንን ኃጢአት ሁሉ አስወግደን፤ በፊታችን ያለውን ሩጫ በጽናት እንሩጥ። (ዕብራውያን 12፡1)

We are "surrounded by so great a cloud of witnesses" — the saints of Hebrews 11 who actively witness our race of faith.

## In the Compendium

**Q194:** The communion of saints is sharing in holy things — faith, sacraments, charisms. Its root is charity, which "does not seek its own" (1 Cor 13:5).

**Q195:** It also means union among holy persons: pilgrims on earth, those being purified, and those in glory — one family in Christ, the Church.
