# Immaculate Conception
**Type:** feast | **Amharic:** ንጽሕተ ልደተ ማርያም | **English:** Immaculate Conception
**Date:** December 8 (Latin Rite)
**Liturgical season:** Advent | **Sources:** 3
**Last updated:** 2026-05-06
**Related:** [[figures/ማርያም]], [[teaching/mary]], [[concepts/ጸጋ]]

## Theological meaning
The Immaculate Conception is the dogma defined by Pope Pius IX in 1854 (Ineffabilis
Deus): the Blessed Virgin Mary, "from the first moment of her conception, by a singular
grace and privilege of almighty God and by virtue of the merits of Jesus Christ, Savior
of the human race, was preserved immune from all stain of original sin." This does not
mean Mary did not need a Savior -- she was saved in advance by the merits of Christ's
Passion. The Catholic Church celebrates this feast on December 8.

## Liturgical observance
White vestments. The Gospel reading is Luke 1:26-28 (the Annunciation). In Ethiopian
Catholic practice, this feast was introduced with Latin influence and is not as deeply
rooted in Ethiopian tradition as the Filsata (Assumption) or the Nativity of Mary.
However, Ethiopian Catholics honor Mary's purity and holiness throughout the year.
