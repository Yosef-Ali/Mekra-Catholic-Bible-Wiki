# ጸሎተ ሐሙስ (Holy Thursday)

**Type:** feast
**Amharic:** ጸሎተ ሐሙስ

## Synthesis

ጸሎተ ሐሙስ (Holy Thursday) commemorates three foundational events of the same evening:

1. **የቁርባን መሠረት (Institution of the Eucharist)** — At the Last Supper, Jesus took bread and wine and said: «ይህ ስለእናንተ የሚሰጥ ሥጋዬ ነው... ይህን ለእኔ መታሰቢያ አድርጉት» (Q273). This is the origin of the Mass/ቅዳሴ.

2. **የክህነት መሠረት (Institution of the Priesthood)** — By commanding "ይህን ለእኔ መታሰቢያ አድርጉት" ("Do this in memory of me"), Jesus ordained the apostles as the first priests, giving them the authority to celebrate the Eucharist (Q339).

3. **የእግር ማጠብ (Washing of Feet)** — Jesus washed the feet of the twelve apostles as an act of humble service, commanding them to do the same for one another (John 13:1–17). This is the origin of the Mandatum ceremony.

The Compendium teaches that the Eucharist is **ቁርባን የክርስቲያናዊ ሕይወት ሁሉ ምንጭና ቁንጮ** — "the source and summit of all Christian life" (Q274). Holy Thursday is the annual commemoration of the moment this gift was given.

## Ethiopian Catholic observance

### ቅዳሴ ሐሙስ (Evening Mass of the Lord's Supper)
- The only Mass celebrated on this day, typically in the evening.
- The Gloria is sung; after it, church bells are silenced until the Easter Vigil.
- The readings recall the Passover (Exodus 12), Paul's account of the Last Supper (1 Corinthians 11:23–26), and the Washing of Feet (John 13:1–15).

### የእግር ማጠብ (Mandatum / Foot Washing)
- The celebrant (priest or bishop) washes the feet of 12 members of the congregation.
- In Ethiopian tradition, this is performed with great solemnity and often with traditional chant.

### ሰዓታት / Adoration at the Altar of Repose
- After Mass, the Blessed Sacrament is processed to a side altar and exposed for adoration.
- The faithful keep vigil through the night, meditating on the agony in Gethsemane (Matthew 26:36–46).
- The main altar is stripped bare, signifying the stripping of Jesus before the Crucifixion.

### ጾም (Fasting)
- The strict Lenten fast continues: no meat, no dairy, no eggs.
- Many faithful fast completely until after the evening liturgy.

## Ethiopian Orthodox parallel

The Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahedo Church celebrates the same day as **ጸሎተ ሐሙስ** with very similar observances. The foot washing ceremony and night vigil are shared traditions rooted in the common Ethiopian Christian heritage predating the Catholic-Orthodox distinction. Both traditions use the Ge'ez liturgical language for parts of the service.

## Triduum context

Holy Thursday opens the **Sacred Triduum** (Triduum Sacrum / የሦስት ቀን ሐዘን):

| Day | Amharic | Event |
|---|---|---|
| Thursday evening | ጸሎተ ሐሙስ | Last Supper, Institution of Eucharist |
| Friday | ስቅለት (Good Friday) | Crucifixion and Death of Christ |
| Saturday | ቅዳሜ ስቡህ (Holy Saturday) | Christ in the tomb, Harrowing of Hell |
| Sunday | ፋሲካ (Easter) | Resurrection |

> «ኢየሱስ ቁርባንን የመሠረተው፣ የመጨረሻውን እራት ከሐዋርያቱ ጋር በበላበትና አልፎ በተሰጠበት ሌሊት ሐሙስ ዕለት ነው።» — Compendium Q272
