## The prayer

> «አባታችን ሆይ፣ በሰማያት የምትኖር —
> ስምህ ይቀደስ፤
> መንግሥትህ ትምጣ፤
> ፈቃድህ በሰማይ እንደ ሆነች —
> እንዲሁም በምድር ትሁን።
> የዕለት እንጀራችንን ስጠን ዛሬ፤
> በደላችንን ይቅር በለን —
> እኛም የበደሉንን ይቅር እንደምንል፤
> ወደ ፈተና አታግባን —
> ከክፉ አድነን እንጂ።
> [መንግሥት ያንተ ናትና፣
> ኃይልና ክብርም ለዘላለሙ።]
> አሜን።»

## Source

- ማቴዎስ 6:9–13 (the Sermon on the Mount, fuller version)
- ሉቃስ 11:2–4 (a shorter form, given to a disciple's request)

## Why this prayer matters

Tertullian called it *"the summary of the whole Gospel."* St. Thomas Aquinas: *"The most perfect of prayers."* It contains every right desire of the Christian heart, in seven petitions, in the order Christ himself taught.

## The seven petitions briefly

1. **Hallowed be thy name** — God's holiness honored on earth as in heaven.
2. **Thy kingdom come** — the reign of Christ established in our hearts and the world.
3. **Thy will be done** — submission, not resignation; loving alignment with God.
4. **Give us this day our daily bread** — material needs, *and* the Eucharist (the "supersubstantial bread").
5. **Forgive us our trespasses** — and the iron condition: *as we forgive*.
6. **Lead us not into temptation** — better translated *"do not let us yield"* — God tests but never tempts to evil.
7. **Deliver us from evil** — from the Evil One personally, not just abstract evil.

For the full theological exposition, see [[teaching/the-lords-prayer]].

## When to pray it

- **At every Mass** — central to the Communion rite.
- **In the morning offering and at night.**
- **In the Rosary** — at the start of each decade.
- **Always** — when no other words come, this is the prayer Christ himself taught.
