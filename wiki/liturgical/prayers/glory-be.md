## The prayer

> «ስብሐት ለአብ ወወልድ ወመንፈስ ቅዱስ —
> ይሁን ዛሬና ምን ጊዜም —
> ለዘላለመ ዓለም።
> አሜን።»

## Why we pray it

This is the **doxology** — the Church's brief hymn of praise to the Holy Trinity. Every prayer, every action, every life is rightly ordered toward this end: the glory of the Father, the Son, and the Holy Spirit.

The prayer comes directly from the worship of the early Church. The first half — *"Glory to the Father, and to the Son, and to the Holy Spirit"* — echoes the baptismal formula given by Christ (ማቴዎስ 28:19) and was used at the end of psalms in Christian prayer from the 2nd century. The second half — *"as it was in the beginning, is now, and ever shall be"* — was added in the 4th century to refute Arian heresies that denied the eternal divinity of the Son.

## When to pray

- **At the end of every psalm** in the Liturgy of the Hours.
- **After each decade of the Rosary** (closing the decade with the Trinity).
- **As a brief prayer** any time of day.
- **At the end of every prayer**, properly understood, since all true prayer rises to the Father through the Son in the Spirit.
