# የመስቀል መንገድ — Stations of the Cross

**Type:** prayer
**Amharic:** የመስቀል መንገድ
**Latin:** Via Crucis / Via Dolorosa
**CCC:** 1674
**Sources:** 4
**Last updated:** 2026-05-09
**Related:** [[teaching/passion-death-resurrection]], [[liturgical/feasts/ስቅለት]], [[liturgical/seasons/Lent]], [[concepts/መስቀል]]

## What it is

A meditative pilgrimage along the path Christ walked from condemnation to burial. Originally walked physically in Jerusalem; in churches worldwide, **fourteen images mounted on the walls** allow the faithful to walk the same path in spirit.

Traditionally prayed on **Fridays** (especially in Lent), and most solemnly on **ስቅለት (Good Friday)**.

## At each station

The standard pattern at each of the 14 stations:

1. **Genuflect** before the image.
2. Say:
   > «እንሰግድልሃለን፣ ክርስቶስ ሆይ፣ እንባርክሃለንም።
   > በቅዱስ መስቀልህ ዓለምን አድነሃልና።»
   > "We adore you, O Christ, and we praise you. Because by your holy cross you have redeemed the world."
3. Read the Scripture passage. Meditate briefly.
4. Pray **Our Father, Hail Mary, Glory Be**.
5. Move to the next station.

## The Fourteen Stations

### 1. Jesus is condemned to death
*ኢየሱስ ለሞት ተፈረደበት*
**ማቴዎስ 27:22–26.** Pilate hands him over. Reflect: how often have I condemned others without truth, or feared the crowd more than God?

### 2. Jesus carries his cross
*ኢየሱስ መስቀሉን ተሸከመ*
**ዮሐንስ 19:17.** He embraces what crushes him. Reflect: what cross am I refusing to carry?

### 3. Jesus falls the first time
*ኢየሱስ ለመጀመሪያ ጊዜ ወደቀ*
*(Tradition.)* The weight overcomes him. Reflect: when I fall in sin, do I stay down or rise again?

### 4. Jesus meets his mother
*ኢየሱስ እናቱን አገኘ*
*(Tradition.)* Two pure hearts pierced by the same sword. Reflect: who suffers with me — and with whom should I be present?

### 5. Simon of Cyrene helps Jesus carry the cross
*የቀሬናው ስምዖን መስቀል ተሸከመ*
**ማቴዎስ 27:32.** Forced into Christ's labor, he becomes a saint. Reflect: do I carry only my own burden, or my neighbor's?

### 6. Veronica wipes the face of Jesus
*ቬሮኒካ የኢየሱስን ፊት አጸዳች*
*(Tradition.)* A small kindness leaves an eternal image. Reflect: have I dared a small mercy when others held back?

### 7. Jesus falls the second time
*ኢየሱስ ለሁለተኛ ጊዜ ወደቀ*
*(Tradition.)* The same sin, again. Reflect: have I given up on rising from a recurring sin?

### 8. Jesus meets the women of Jerusalem
*ኢየሱስ የኢየሩሳሌምን ሴቶች አገኘ*
**ሉቃስ 23:27–31.** "Weep not for me but for yourselves." Reflect: do I mourn only my own losses while ignoring my soul?

### 9. Jesus falls the third time
*ኢየሱስ ለሦስተኛ ጊዜ ወደቀ*
*(Tradition.)* On the very threshold of Calvary. Reflect: even the third fall did not stop him; it must not stop me.

### 10. Jesus is stripped of his garments
*ኢየሱስ ልብሱ ተገፈፈ*
**ዮሐንስ 19:23–24.** Total exposure. Reflect: what self-image must I let go of?

### 11. Jesus is nailed to the cross
*ኢየሱስ በመስቀል ላይ ተቸነከረ*
**ሉቃስ 23:33.** Hands and feet — the instruments of will and motion — pierced. Reflect: are my hands serving God? My feet running where he sends?

### 12. Jesus dies on the cross
*ኢየሱስ በመስቀል ላይ ሞተ*
**ዮሐንስ 19:30.** "ተፈጸመ።" — *"It is finished."* Pause. Kneel. The redemption of the world.

### 13. Jesus is taken down from the cross
*ኢየሱስ ከመስቀል ወረደ*
**ዮሐንስ 19:38.** Mary receives the dead body of her Son — the *Pietà*. Reflect: how do I receive grief?

### 14. Jesus is laid in the tomb
*ኢየሱስ በመቃብር ተቀመጠ*
**ማቴዎስ 27:59–60.** Sealed in stone. The waiting begins. Reflect: do I trust God's silent work?

### *(15. The Resurrection)*
*ትንሣኤ*
Some modern devotions add a fifteenth station — **the Resurrection** (ማቴ. 28:1–10) — so the meditation ends with hope, not the tomb.

## Closing prayer

> «ጌታዬ ኢየሱስ ክርስቶስ —
> በዚህ ቅዱስ የመከራህ መንገድ የተራመድኩበትን አስታውስ።
> በመስቀልህ ኃይል —
> ኃጢአቴን ይቅር በለኝ።
> በትንሣኤህ ብርሃን —
> ሕይወቴን አድስ።
> በፍጻሜዬ ሰዓት —
> ከእርስዋ ጋር በሰማይ መንግሥት ተቀበለኝ።
> አሜን።»

## When to pray

- **Every Friday** in Lent — the most traditional time.
- **Good Friday (ስቅለት)** afternoon — usually communally at 3:00 PM (the hour of Christ's death).
- **Personal devotion** — any day, any season. The path is always available.
- **In sickness or grief** — when no other prayer comes, walk the road with him.

## The indulgence

The Stations of the Cross prayed devoutly carry a **plenary indulgence** under the usual conditions (sacramental confession, Eucharistic communion, prayer for the Pope's intentions, detachment from all sin). A deep practice of repentance sealed in mercy.
