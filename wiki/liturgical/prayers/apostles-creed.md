## Origin

Called "Apostles' Creed" because tradition holds it summarizes the apostles' own teaching. It developed from the **baptismal interrogations** of the early Roman Church (2nd–4th c.) — the questions asked of catechumens at the moment of baptism: *"Do you believe in God the Father almighty?"* — *"Do you believe in Jesus Christ his Son?"* — *"Do you believe in the Holy Spirit?"*

It is the **shorter and older** of the two great creeds. The longer **Nicene Creed** (325/381 AD) is used at Mass; the Apostles' Creed is used at baptism, in the Rosary, and in personal prayer.

## Structure — three articles

The creed is organized around the **Three Persons of the Holy Trinity**:

1. **Article 1** — God the Father, Creator of all.
2. **Article 2** — Jesus Christ the Son: incarnate, crucified, risen, ascended, returning.
3. **Article 3** — The Holy Spirit, the Church, the communion of saints, forgiveness, resurrection, eternal life.

## When to pray

- **In every baptism** — the central profession of faith.
- **At the start of every Rosary.**
- **In the renewal of baptismal promises** — at the Easter Vigil, on the anniversary of baptism.
- **As a profession of faith** before death.
- **As a daily morning prayer** — to remember whose I am.
