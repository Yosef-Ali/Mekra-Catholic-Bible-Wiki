## Why pray at meals

Christ himself **gave thanks** before he broke bread (ማቴ. 14:19, ማቴ. 26:26). He blessed food, and through it taught us that **all food comes from the Father** — and that the table of bread points to the table of the Eucharist. To eat without thanksgiving is to forget who feeds us.

A Christian table is always a small altar. The grace before meals is the most ordinary prayer in the world — and one of the most important, because the home is the **first Church**.

## Before meals (ከምግብ በፊት)

> «በስመ አብ ወወልድ ወመንፈስ ቅዱስ።
>
> ጌታ ሆይ — ባርከን —
> እነዚህ ስጦታዎችህም ይባርኩ —
> ከልግሥናህ የተቀበልነው።
> በክርስቶስ ጌታችን።
> አሜን።»

> *In the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Spirit.
> Bless us, O Lord, and these your gifts which we are about to receive
> from your bounty. Through Christ our Lord. Amen.*

## After meals (ከምግብ በኋላ)

> «ቸር አምላክ — ስለ ስጦታዎችህ ሁሉ —
> እናመሰግናለን —
> ለዘላለመ ዓለም በሕይወት የምትኖር።
> አሜን።
>
> በታማኝ ሰዎች ነፍሶች —
> በእግዚአብሔር ምሕረት —
> በሰላም ያድሩ።
> አሜን።»

> *We give you thanks for all your benefits, O Almighty God,
> who lives and reigns forever. Amen.
> May the souls of the faithful departed,
> through the mercy of God, rest in peace. Amen.*

## A simple morning meal grace

For breakfast or quick meals — a single line:

> «ጌታ ሆይ — ስለዚህ እንጀራ — አመሰግንሃለሁ።»
> *"Lord, thank you for this bread."*

## A grace remembering the hungry

For families teaching children that the table is also where we remember those without:

> «ቸር አምላክ — ስለዚህ ምግብ እናመሰግናለን።
> ምግብ ለሌላቸው ወንድሞቻችንና እህቶቻችንም — ፍላጎታቸውን አሟላ።
> በልብና በእጃችን — ስጦታህን ለሌሎች ልናካፍል — አስችለን።
> አሜን።»

## Tips for the family table

- **The head of the family leads** — father, mother, or eldest. Children may take turns.
- **All hands joined or folded** — visible reverence.
- **Wait until grace is finished** before any food is taken — even a sip of water.
- **Don't rush.** A 20-second prayer changes the whole meal.
- **Sunday meal especially** — make grace solemn. The Lord's Day extends to the table.

## A note on Christian hospitality

When non-Catholics or non-Christians eat with you, pray grace anyway — kindly, briefly, without drama. Your prayer is simply your home's character; no one is being made to participate against their will. Many guests appreciate that food is sacred to you, even if their tradition is different.
