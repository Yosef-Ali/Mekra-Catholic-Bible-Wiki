## What it is

A **litany** is a series of short petitions, each followed by a fixed response. The Litany of the Blessed Virgin Mary — also called the **Litany of Loreto** (from the Italian shrine where it was prayed continuously from the 16th century) — is a meditation on Mary's titles drawn from Scripture, the Fathers, and Church tradition.

It is most often prayed at the **end of the Rosary**, in **May** (Mary's month), and during **family prayer**.

## How to pray

The leader says each invocation; all respond. The same response is used through entire sections — listen for when it changes.

## When to pray

- **At the end of the Rosary** — the traditional pairing.
- **In the month of May** — Marian devotion.
- **On Saturdays** — traditionally Mary's day.
- **At the Feast of the Assumption (ፍልሰታ)** and other Marian feasts.
- **In times of distress** — Mary as Help of Christians has answered countless prayers.

## A note on the titles

Some titles may seem strange — *Tower of Ivory*, *House of Gold*, *Ark of the Covenant*. These come from the **Song of Songs** and **Old Testament typology**:

- **Ark of the Covenant** — Mary carried in her womb the Word of God, just as the Ark carried the tablets of the Law and the manna.
- **Gate of Heaven** — Christ entered the world through her.
- **Morning Star** — she heralds the rising Sun of Justice.

Each title is a window into a different facet of her role in salvation. The litany is not magic; it is the Church's way of teaching theology through prayer.
