## Origin

This is the longer and more theologically precise of the two great creeds. It was forged in the fires of two ecumenical councils:

- **First Council of Nicaea (325 AD)** — answered the **Arian** heresy that denied the full divinity of the Son. Hence the explicit phrasing: *"true God from true God — begotten, not made — one in being (homoousios) with the Father."*
- **First Council of Constantinople (381 AD)** — completed the third article on the Holy Spirit, against the **Macedonian** heresy that denied the Spirit's divinity. Hence: *"Lord and giver of life… who together with the Father and the Son is adored and glorified."*

## A note on the Filioque (ፊሊዮክዌ)

The Latin (Catholic) version says the Holy Spirit proceeds *"from the Father **and the Son**"* (Filioque). The Greek/Ethiopian Orthodox version says only *"from the Father."*

The **Ethiopian Catholic Geez Rite preserves the original "from the Father" formulation** for liturgical continuity, while teaching the same theological truth the Latin formula expresses: that the Spirit is sent into the world *through* the Son, who is *consubstantial* with the Father.

This is one of the most-discussed differences between Catholic and Orthodox tradition. See [[comparative/the-creed]].

## When to pray

- **At every Sunday Mass** and on solemnities — between the homily and the Prayer of the Faithful.
- **In personal study** — meditating slowly on each line.
- **In RCIA** — handed over to catechumens during Lent.

For the theology of each article in detail, see [[teaching/the-creed]] and the related teaching pages on the Trinity, Christ, the Holy Spirit, and the Church.
