## Catholic teaching
The Catholic Church teaches seven sacraments instituted by Christ: Baptism, Confirmation,
Eucharist, Penance, Anointing of the Sick, Holy Orders, and Matrimony (CCC 1210-1211).
They are efficacious signs of grace by which divine life is dispensed. The Eucharist is
"the source and summit" toward which all sacraments are ordered. The Council of Trent
(Session VII, 1547) dogmatically defined the septenary number against Protestant
reduction to two.

## Eastern Orthodox position
Also seven mysteries. The Orthodox emphasize the Church as the fundamental sacrament,
with individual mysteries as expressions of the Church's sacramental life.

## Protestant positions
Most recognise only two (Baptism, Eucharist) since only these have explicit dominical
institution. Anglicans/Lutherans retain some of the other five but not as sacraments.
The EOTC-Catholic agreement on seven is significant ecumenically.

## Where the differences come from
The septenary number was settled in the West by Peter Lombard (12th c.) and defined at
Trent (1547). The EOTC never participated in Trent but through shared patristic
tradition also acknowledges seven -- an independent confirmation of the same conclusion.
