## Catholic teaching
The sixth commandment ("You shall not commit adultery") and the ninth ("You shall not
covet your neighbor's wife") govern human sexuality and purity of heart (CCC 2331-2400,
2514-2533). Sexuality is ordered to conjugal love between husband and wife and to
procreation. Chastity is the virtue that integrates sexuality within the person.
Pornography, masturbation, fornication, and homosexual acts are contrary to chastity.
The ninth commandment forbids interior consent to impure desires.

## Eastern Orthodox position
Similar moral teaching on chastity, adultery, and purity of heart. The Orthodox are
less likely to produce detailed casuistry on sexual matters, emphasizing instead the
transformative power of asceticism and the sacraments in forming chaste persons.

## Protestant positions
Most Protestant traditions teach marital fidelity and chastity. Liberal Protestantism
has shifted significantly on premarital sex, cohabitation, and same-sex relationships
in recent decades -- creating a growing divergence from Catholic-EOTC teaching.
Conservative evangelicals maintain traditional sexual ethics with varying degrees of
"purity culture" emphasis.

## Where the differences come from
The sixth/ninth commandment ethics are substantially shared across apostolic churches.
Modern divergence stems from the sexual revolution's impact on Western Protestantism.
The EOTC and Ethiopian Catholic churches have been less affected by these Western
cultural shifts, though globalization is changing this.
