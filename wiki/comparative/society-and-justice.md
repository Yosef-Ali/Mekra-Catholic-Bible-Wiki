## Catholic teaching
The human person is by nature a social being and needs life in society (CCC 1879).
Catholic social teaching (CST) is a developed body of doctrine addressing economic
justice, the common good, subsidiarity, solidarity, the dignity of work, and care for
creation. From Rerum Novarum (1891) to Fratelli Tutti (2020), modern popes have
articulated a systematic social magisterium.

## Eastern Orthodox position
The Orthodox have a less developed social teaching corpus than Catholicism. The "social
ethos" of the Orthodox Church is more implicit in liturgy and patristic texts. Recent
patriarchal encyclicals (especially by the Ecumenical Patriarch on ecology) have begun
to address modernity more directly.

## Protestant positions
Protestant social engagement ranges from the Social Gospel (liberal) to quietism (some
evangelicals) to dominion theology (Reconstructionists). Reformed traditions have a
developed theology of cultural engagement (Abraham Kuyper's "every square inch").

## Where the differences come from
The Catholic CST tradition is uniquely systematic, driven by the papacy's teaching office.
The EOTC's social role has been shaped by Ethiopia's unique history as the only African
nation to resist colonization. The Soviet-aligned Derg regime (1974-1991) suppressed
church social activity, which has only recently been rebuilt.
