## Catholic teaching
Marriage is a sacrament -- a covenant by which a man and woman establish a partnership
for the whole of life, ordered toward the good of the spouses and the procreation and
education of children (CCC 1601). Christ raised marriage to sacramental dignity. Its
essential properties are unity and indissolubility -- a valid, consummated sacramental
marriage cannot be dissolved by any human power (CCC 1640). Annulment is a declaration
that no valid marriage bond ever existed, not "Catholic divorce."

## Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahedo (EOTC) position

## Eastern Orthodox position
The Orthodox permit divorce/remarriage in cases of adultery and other grave circumstances
based on the "Matthean exception" (Matthew 19:9) and the principle of economia (pastoral
discretion). Second marriages use a penitential rite. The Orthodox view marriage as
indissoluble in principle but acknowledge sin can destroy the relationship.

## Protestant positions
Most Protestants permit divorce/remarriage, especially on grounds of adultery or
abandonment. Few consider marriage a sacrament -- it is a covenant before God but not
sacramental. Some conservative evangelicals restrict divorce to adultery alone.

## Where the differences come from
Catholic indissolubility was clarified at Trent (Session XXIV, 1563). The Eastern (EOTC)
position reflects an earlier interpretation of Matthew 19:9. Ethiopia's ጥሎሽ has
pre-Christian roots, now integrated into Christian marriage custom.
