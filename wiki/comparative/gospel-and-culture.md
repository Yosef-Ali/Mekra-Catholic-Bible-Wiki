## Catholic teaching
The Catholic Church teaches that faith and culture are distinct but not opposed -- the
Gospel purifies and elevates what is good in every culture (Gaudium et Spes 58; CCC
1204-1206). Inculturation is the process by which the Gospel takes flesh in particular
cultures without losing its identity. The Ethiopian Catholic Church (Eastern Rite) is
a living example -- maintaining the Ge'ez liturgical tradition, Ethiopian fasting
practices, and cultural customs while in full communion with Rome.

## Eastern Orthodox position
The Orthodox concept of "incarnation of the Gospel" in culture parallels the Catholic
approach, though with stronger emphasis on the national church model (each nation has
its own autocephalous church with its own liturgical language and customs).

## Protestant positions
Protestant missions have varied: some emphasized radical cultural transformation
(rejecting indigenous customs as pagan), others pioneered contextualization (translating
Scripture, adapting worship forms). The Ethiopian Kale Heywet and Mekane Yesus churches
(Lutheran-origin) represent a different inculturation model.

## Where the differences come from
The EOTC's 1600-year history in Ethiopia produced Christianity's deepest inculturation
in Africa. The Ethiopian Catholic Church uniquely bridges this tradition with Roman
communion. Protestant missions (19th-20th centuries) brought different approaches.
