## Catholic teaching
Christian prayer is a covenant relationship between God and humanity in Christ (CCC
2558-2565). The Catholic tradition includes vocal prayer, meditation, and contemplation.
The Liturgy of the Hours (Divine Office) sanctifies the day through prayer at appointed
hours -- Lauds, Vespers, Compline, and the minor hours. The Catechism identifies five
forms: blessing/adoration, petition, intercession, thanksgiving, and praise (CCC
2626-2643).

## Eastern Orthodox position
The Horologion (Book of Hours) parallels the Sa'atat. The Jesus Prayer is central to
hesychast spirituality. Byzantine chant parallels Yared's Mahlet as liturgical music.

## Protestant positions
Protestant prayer emphasizes spontaneity. Liturgical traditions (Anglican, Lutheran)
retain Daily Office forms. Evangelical/Pentecostal emphasize extemporaneous prayer.
Contemplative traditions largely lost in the Reformation, revived by Catholic influence.

## Where the differences come from
The Hours derive from Jewish practice (Daniel 6:10, Acts 3:1). St. Yared (6th c.)
created the Ethiopian tradition independently. The EOTC's unique three-mode chant system
and Sa'atat represent Christianity's richest African liturgical prayer tradition.
